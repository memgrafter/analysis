# A Family-Based Approach to Safety Cases for Controlled Airspaces in Small Uncrewed Aerial Systems

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2502.02559
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.02559
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Introduces SafeSPLE method for automated safety-case generation for sUAS flights using feature models

## Executive Summary
This paper presents a novel approach to generating safety cases for small Uncrewed Aerial Systems (sUAS) operating in controlled airspace. The Safety Case Software Product Line Engineering (SafeSPLE) method extends product-family techniques to create parameterized safety cases that can be automatically instantiated for specific flight scenarios. The approach uses feature models to represent flight characteristics and hazards, enabling customized safety-case generation for different sUAS models under varying conditions.

The research addresses the growing need for systematic safety assessment as sUAS operations expand into controlled airspace under UTM frameworks. By automating the safety-case generation process, SafeSPLE aims to streamline decision-making for sUAS entry into controlled airspace while ensuring comprehensive safety considerations are addressed for each specific flight scenario.

## Method Summary
The SafeSPLE approach combines safety-case engineering with software product line techniques to create a parameterized safety case framework. The method involves developing a feature model that captures the variability in sUAS operations, including aircraft characteristics, environmental conditions, and operational parameters. This feature model is then used to automatically generate customized safety-case instances for specific flight scenarios.

The validation process involves testing the generated safety cases against specific sUAS models (DJI Mini 4 Pro and DEERC D20) under different weather conditions. The system checks critical parameters such as wind speed, visibility, and battery reserves to ensure flight safety. The approach provides a checklist-based output that can be used by pilots and regulators to verify safe operations before sUAS entry into UTM-controlled airspace.

## Key Results
- SafeSPLE successfully generates valid safety-case instances for specific sUAS models under varying weather conditions
- The feature model effectively captures critical flight parameters including wind speed, visibility, and battery reserves
- Automated safety-case generation provides a systematic checklist for pilots and regulators to verify safe sUAS operations

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
SafeSPLE leverages product-line engineering principles to create reusable safety-case components that can be configured for specific flight scenarios. The feature model acts as a variability mechanism, encoding the relationships between different flight parameters and their impact on safety requirements. This parameterized approach enables rapid generation of customized safety cases while maintaining consistency across different sUAS models and operational conditions.

  - Why needed: Reduces development effort while maintaining safety assurance quality
