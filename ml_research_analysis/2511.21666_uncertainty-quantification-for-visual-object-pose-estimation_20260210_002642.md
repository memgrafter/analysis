# Uncertainty Quantification for Visual Object Pose Estimation

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2511.21666
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.21666
- Authors: Lorenzo Shaikewitz; Charis Georgiou; Luca Carlone
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Introduces SLUE method for statistically rigorous pose uncertainty bounds using S-lemma and SOS hierarchy

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the critical challenge of quantifying uncertainty in monocular object pose estimation, a problem with significant implications for robotics and autonomous systems. The authors propose SLUE (S-Lemma Uncertainty Ellipsoid Estimation), a method that propagates keypoint uncertainty bounds through non-convex pose constraints to obtain joint ellipsoidal uncertainty bounds containing the true pose. The approach leverages the generalized S-lemma and sum-of-squares hierarchy to compute minimum volume bounding ellipsoids, providing statistically rigorous guarantees under minimal distributional assumptions.

## Method Summary
SLUE computes pose uncertainty by first obtaining high-probability bounds on keypoint locations, then propagating these through the non-convex constraints of the pose estimation problem. The core innovation is using a relaxation of the minimum volume bounding ellipsoid problem based on the generalized S-lemma, which can be extended to a sum-of-squares hierarchy guaranteed to converge to the optimal bound. This approach produces interpretable separate bounds for translation and orientation through a projection scheme. The method is designed to be agnostic to the specific pose estimator used and requires only keypoint uncertainty information as input.

## Key Results
- Produces significantly tighter translation bounds (multiple orders of magnitude smaller than baselines)
- Generates competitive orientation bounds while being substantially faster than prior approaches
- Demonstrates effectiveness across three real-world datasets
- Provides statistical guarantees under minimal distributional assumptions

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
SLUE works by systematically propagating uncertainty through the geometric constraints of pose estimation. By using the generalized S-lemma to relax the minimum volume bounding ellipsoid problem, the method can handle the non-convex nature of pose constraints while still guaranteeing that the true pose lies within the computed bounds. The sum-of-squares hierarchy provides a systematic way to tighten these bounds progressively. The separation into translation and orientation bounds via projection allows for more interpretable and actionable uncertainty estimates in practical applications.

## Foundational Learning
1. **Generalized S-lemma** - A fundamental result in optimization theory for handling non-convex constraints; needed to relax the minimum volume bounding ellipsoid problem; quick check: verify the non-negativity of the Lagrange multiplier

2. **Sum-of-Squares (SOS) Hierarchy** - A systematic approach to polynomial optimization; needed to iteratively tighten the uncertainty bounds; quick check: confirm convergence of the hierarchy to the optimal solution

3. **Non-convex Optimization** - The pose estimation problem involves non-convex constraints; needed to understand why relaxation techniques are necessary; quick check: identify the specific non-convex constraints in the pose formulation

4. **Ellipsoidal Uncertainty Representation** - A compact way to represent multidimensional uncertainty; needed to provide interpretable and computationally tractable bounds; quick check: verify the positive definiteness of the ellipsoid shape matrix

## Architecture Onboarding
**Component Map:** Keypoint Detection -> Uncertainty Estimation -> SLUE Processing -> Pose Uncertainty Bounds
**Critical Path:** The method takes keypoint detections with uncertainty bounds as input, applies the SLUE algorithm (S-lemma relaxation + SOS hierarchy), and outputs joint ellipsoidal bounds on translation and orientation
**Design Tradeoffs:** SLUE prioritizes statistical rigor and computational efficiency over absolute tightness of bounds; uses relaxation techniques that guarantee inclusion of true pose at the cost of potentially conservative estimates
**Failure Signatures:** Poor keypoint uncertainty estimates lead to overly conservative or invalid pose bounds; highly non-convex scenarios may require many SOS hierarchy levels, increasing computation time

## Limitations
- Assumes keypoint uncertainty is described by finite high-probability bounds, which may not capture complex real-world distributions
- Quality of final pose bounds heavily depends on accuracy of initial keypoint uncertainty estimates
- Scalability to scenes with many objects or high-resolution inputs is not thoroughly explored

## Confidence
- High confidence in theoretical foundation: S-lemma and SOS hierarchy are well-established optimization techniques
- Medium confidence in practical performance claims: Results demonstrate superiority on benchmark datasets but generalizability remains to be seen
- Low confidence in computational scalability: Method is described as faster than baselines but large-scale performance is not thoroughly examined

## Next Checks
