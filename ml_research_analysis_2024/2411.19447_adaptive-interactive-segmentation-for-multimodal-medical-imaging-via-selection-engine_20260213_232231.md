# Adaptive Interactive Segmentation for Multimodal Medical Imaging via Selection Engine

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2411.19447
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.19447
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Strategy-driven Interactive Segmentation Model (SISeg) achieves robust multi-modal medical imaging segmentation with 10.97% accuracy improvement in Mammography and 6.33% in Endoscopy

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of achieving fast, efficient, and accurate segmentation across multiple medical imaging modalities. The authors propose SISeg, a strategy-driven interactive segmentation model built on SAM2 that integrates an Adaptive Frame Selection Engine (AFSE) to dynamically select optimal prompt frames. The model demonstrates robust adaptability and generalization across 10 datasets covering 7 medical imaging modalities, with significant improvements in segmentation accuracy while reducing annotation burdens in clinical settings.

## Method Summary
The Strategy-driven Interactive Segmentation Model (SISeg) builds on SAM2's interactive segmentation framework and integrates an Adaptive Frame Selection Engine (AFSE) to dynamically select optimal prompt frames. The AFSE computes composite scores based on image features (brightness, contrast, edge density, color histogram similarity, shape similarity) and uses K-means clustering to identify representative frames for annotation. The model employs various prompt types and an unsupervised scoring mechanism to evaluate image features, eliminating the need for extensive prior medical knowledge. The approach is validated across 10 datasets spanning 7 medical imaging modalities using Dice coefficient and Intersection over Union (IoU) metrics.

## Key Results
- SISeg demonstrates robust adaptability and generalization across 10 datasets covering 7 medical imaging modalities
- AFSE outperforms random and uniform strategies with significant accuracy improvements: 10.97% increase in Mammography and 6.33% improvement in Endoscopy
- The model effectively reduces annotation burden while maintaining high segmentation efficiency in clinical settings
- Comprehensive experiments validate the effectiveness of the adaptive frame selection approach across diverse medical imaging contexts
