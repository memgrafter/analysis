# Low-Rank Online Dynamic Assortment with Dual Contextual Information

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2404.17592
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.17592

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of real-time personalized product recommendations in e-commerce using a dynamic assortment problem with dual contextual information (user and item features). The authors introduce a low-rank dynamic assortment model to handle high-dimensional scenarios efficiently. They propose an Explore-Low-rank-Subspace-then-Apply-UCB (ELSA-UCB) algorithm that estimates intrinsic subspaces and uses the upper confidence bound approach to balance exploration and exploitation. The method achieves a regret bound of O((d1 + d2)r√T), where d1 and d2 are the dimensions of user and item features, r is the rank of the parameter matrix, and T is the time horizon. This represents a significant improvement over prior methods. The approach is validated through extensive simulations and an application to the Expedia hotel recommendation dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness in real-world scenarios.

## Method Summary
The paper proposes a low-rank dynamic assortment model with dual contextual information, where the preference matrix is assumed to have a low-rank structure. The authors introduce the ELSA-UCB algorithm, which consists of two phases: an exploration phase and an exploitation phase. In the exploration phase, the algorithm estimates the intrinsic subspaces of the preference matrix using random projections. In the exploitation phase, the algorithm uses the upper confidence bound approach to select the optimal assortment based on the estimated subspaces. The regret analysis shows that the ELSA-UCB algorithm achieves a regret bound of O((d1 + d2)r√T), which is a significant improvement over prior methods. The performance of the proposed method is evaluated through extensive simulations and a real-world application to the Expedia hotel recommendation dataset.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The low-rank assumption on the preference matrix allows for efficient estimation of the intrinsic subspaces, reducing the dimensionality of the problem. The ELSA-UCB algorithm balances exploration and exploitation by first exploring the subspace and then applying the upper confidence bound approach to select the optimal assortment. This approach leads to a significant reduction in regret compared to prior methods.

## Limitations
- The low-rank assumption on the preference matrix may not hold in all real-world scenarios.
- The regret analysis assumes a fixed parameter matrix, which may not capture the dynamic nature of user preferences and item characteristics.
- The performance of the ELSA-UCB algorithm is sensitive to the choice of hyperparameters, such as the confidence bound scaling factor.

## Confidence
- High: The regret bound of O((d1 + d2)r√T) is correctly derived and represents a significant improvement over prior methods under the stated assumptions.
- Medium: The effectiveness of the proposed method is demonstrated through simulations and a real-world application, but the generalizability of these results to other domains and datasets is uncertain.
- Low: The low-rank assumption and the fixed parameter matrix assumption may not hold in all practical scenarios, which could impact the performance of the proposed method.
