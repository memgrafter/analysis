# SDI-Net: Toward Sufficient Dual-View Interaction for Low-light Stereo Image Enhancement

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2408.10934
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.10934
- Authors: Linlin Hu; Ao Sun; Shijie Hao; Richang Hong; Meng Wang
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Develops SDI-Net leveraging cross-view information for low-light stereo image enhancement, achieving state-of-the-art performance on public datasets

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of enhancing low-light stereo images by developing a model called SDI-Net. Unlike most existing methods that focus on single-view enhancement, SDI-Net leverages cross-view information through a novel Cross-View Sufficient Interaction Module (CSIM). The model demonstrates superior performance on public datasets, with significant improvements in PSNR and SSIM values compared to previous methods, effectively restoring illumination and details in low-light stereo images.

## Method Summary
SDI-Net is designed to enhance low-light stereo images by leveraging cross-view information. The core of the model is the Cross-View Sufficient Interaction Module (CSIM), which consists of two key components: the Cross-View Attention Interaction Module (CAIM) for aligning features between left and right views, and the Pixel and Channel Attention Block (PCAB) for refining features at multiple levels. This approach enables the model to effectively utilize information from both views, leading to improved enhancement results.

## Key Results
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on public datasets for low-light stereo image enhancement
- Significantly higher PSNR and SSIM values compared to previous methods
- Demonstrates effectiveness in restoring illumination and details in low-light stereo images

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The effectiveness of SDI-Net stems from its ability to leverage cross-view information through the Cross-View Sufficient Interaction Module (CSIM). By aligning features between left and right views using the Cross-View Attention Interaction Module (CAIM) and refining them with the Pixel and Channel Attention Block (PCAB), the model can effectively utilize complementary information from both views. This approach addresses the limitations of single-view enhancement methods and leads to improved restoration of illumination and details in low-light stereo images.
