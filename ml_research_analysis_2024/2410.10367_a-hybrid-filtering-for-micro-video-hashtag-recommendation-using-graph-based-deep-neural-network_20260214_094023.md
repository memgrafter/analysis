# A Hybrid Filtering for Micro-video Hashtag Recommendation using Graph-based Deep Neural Network

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2410.10367
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.10367
- Reference count: 14
- Proposes a hybrid filtering approach using graph-based deep neural network for micro-video hashtag recommendation

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces MISHON, a hybrid filtering approach for micro-video hashtag recommendation that combines content-based and collaborative filtering techniques. The method leverages a graph-based deep neural network to model complex interactions between users and multimodal content (visual, acoustic, textual) through user-to-user, modality-to-modality, and user-to-modality edges. The model achieves significant improvements in hashtag recommendation performance, with 3.6-6.5% relative improvement in F1-score over state-of-the-art methods, and specifically addresses the cold-start user problem with a 15.8% relative improvement in F1-score.

## Method Summary
The approach extracts modality-specific features using pre-trained models (ViT for visual, Wav2Vec2.0 for acoustic, BERT for textual), applies attention mechanisms to identify important features, and constructs an interaction graph with four node types and seven edge types. GraphSAGE is used to propagate and aggregate information, refining the embeddings. For cold-start users, a social influence technique recommends hashtags based on popular users' behavior. The final hashtag predictions are made using a dense layer with softmax activation, trained with cross-entropy loss.

## Key Results
- Achieves 3.6%, 2.8%, and 6.5% relative improvement in F1-score compared to state-of-the-art methods on TMALL, INSVIDEO, and YFCC datasets respectively
- Addresses cold-start user problem with 15.8% relative improvement in F1-score over content-only approach
- Demonstrates effectiveness of graph-based neural network in capturing multimodal interactions for hashtag recommendation

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: The graph-based deep neural network effectively captures and refines modality-specific and user representations by modeling user-to-user, modality-to-modality, and user-to-modality interactions.
- Mechanism: By constructing a graph with four types of nodes (visual, acoustic, textual modalities, and users) and seven types of edges, the model uses GraphSAGE to propagate and aggregate information from neighboring nodes, thereby enriching the embeddings.
- Core assumption: The interactions between nodes in the graph are meaningful and can be used to improve the representation of each node.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "We employ a graph-based deep neural network to model user to user, modality to modality, and user to modality interactions."
  - [section] "We construct an undirected graph G = (N, E), where N and E denote the collection of vertices and edges, respectively."
  - [corpus] Weak - no direct evidence in corpus neighbors.
- Break condition: If the similarity measures used for edge construction (cosine similarity for modalities, Jaccard for users) do not accurately reflect the true relationships, the model's performance will degrade.

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: The attention mechanism filters out noisy information and highlights the most relevant features in each modality for hashtag recommendation.
- Mechanism: The attention mechanism assigns weights to each unit in the modality-specific features based on their importance for hashtag recommendation, and the weighted sum is used to obtain an enriched representation.
- Core assumption: Certain features in each modality are more important than others for determining the relevance of hashtags.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "We then employ an attention mechanism on the modality-specific features to find information that is most apt to recommend hashtags."
  - [section] "Due to the effectiveness of attention mechanism (Vaswani et al., 2017), we apply it individually on every modality as given in Equation 6."
  - [corpus] Weak - no direct evidence in corpus neighbors.
- Break condition: If the attention mechanism fails to correctly identify the important features, the enriched representations will be suboptimal, leading to poor hashtag recommendations.

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: The social influence technique effectively addresses the cold-start user problem by recommending hashtags based on the behavior of influential users.
- Mechanism: The model identifies popular users based on their engagement rate and recommends hashtags used by these users to cold-start users, assuming that new users will mimic the behavior of popular users to gain social attention.
- Core assumption: Cold-start users are likely to use hashtags similar to those used by popular users in order to increase their content's visibility.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "we also propose a social influence and content-based technique to model the relatedness of cold-start users with influential users."
  - [section] "We devise Algorithm 1 to construct user-user edges and associate cold-start users with other users, assuming that cold-start users tend to utilize hashtags as used by the most popular users to increase their content's visibility and garner attention from other users on that platform."
  - [corpus] Weak - no direct evidence in corpus neighbors.
- Break condition: If the engagement rate is not a good indicator of influence, or if cold-start users do not actually mimic the behavior of popular users, the technique will fail to provide relevant recommendations.

## Limitations
- Computational complexity of graph-based approach may not scale efficiently to platforms with millions of active users and videos
- Reliance on pre-trained models assumes these models are well-suited to micro-video content across all domains
- Cold-start user solution depends heavily on the assumption that new users mimic popular users' hashtag behavior

## Confidence

- **High confidence**: The experimental results showing F1-score improvements over baseline methods, as these are directly measurable and reported with specific numbers.
- **Medium confidence**: The claimed mechanisms for how the graph-based neural network improves hashtag recommendation, as these are theoretically sound but not extensively validated through ablation studies.
- **Low confidence**: The social influence technique's effectiveness across diverse social media platforms, as it relies on behavioral assumptions that may not hold universally.
