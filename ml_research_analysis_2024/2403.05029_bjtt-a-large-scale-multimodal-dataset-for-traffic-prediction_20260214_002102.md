# BjTT: A Large-scale Multimodal Dataset for Traffic Prediction

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2403.05029
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.05029
- Authors: Chengyang Zhang; Yong Zhang; Qitan Shao; Jiangtao Feng; Bo Li; Yisheng Lv; Xinglin Piao; Baocai Yin
- Reference count: 33
- Primary result: Introduces BjTT, a large-scale multimodal traffic dataset with 32,000+ text-traffic pairs from Beijing, demonstrating improved traffic prediction through integration of textual event descriptions.

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces BjTT, a large-scale multimodal dataset for traffic prediction that addresses limitations of existing datasets by incorporating textual event descriptions alongside traffic data. The dataset includes over 32,000 text-traffic pairs covering three months of traffic information from more than 1,200 roads in Beijing, with each traffic record paired with text describing the time, location, and type of events occurring on the road. The authors evaluate several state-of-the-art traffic prediction methods and a text-guided generative model on the BjTT dataset. Results show that traditional traffic prediction methods struggle with long-term predictions and are insensitive to abnormal events, while the text-guided generative model can effectively generate realistic traffic situations from textual inputs. The dataset and experimental findings highlight the potential of multimodal data in improving traffic prediction accuracy and addressing real-world challenges in intelligent transportation systems.

## Method Summary
The paper introduces BjTT, a multimodal dataset containing over 32,000 text-traffic pairs from Beijing's road network. Traffic data includes velocity and congestion levels collected from more than 1,200 major roads over three months. Each traffic record is paired with textual descriptions of events occurring at specific times and locations. The authors evaluate six state-of-the-art traffic prediction methods (STGCN, GWN, ASTGCN, STSGCN, MTGNN, STG-NCDE) and one text-guided generative model (LDM) on this dataset. Models are trained using a 70% training, 10% validation, and 20% test split. Performance is measured using MAE and RMSE metrics, with particular attention to long-term prediction capabilities and sensitivity to abnormal events.

## Key Results
- Traditional traffic prediction methods show inverse relationship between prediction time range and accuracy, with long-term predictions being particularly unreliable
- Text-guided generative models effectively generate realistic traffic situations from textual inputs, demonstrating better handling of abnormal events
- BjTT represents the largest publicly available multimodal traffic dataset, with over 32,000 text-traffic pairs covering diverse traffic patterns and events
- Traffic prediction methods show varying sensitivity to abnormal events, with some models being significantly less responsive than others

## Limitations

- Dataset evaluation relies heavily on synthetic text generation for event descriptions, which may not capture full complexity of real-world traffic events
- The dataset only covers a three-month period in Beijing, potentially limiting generalizability to other geographic regions or time periods
- 80% semantic similarity threshold for generated texts may not fully capture nuanced relationships between textual descriptions and actual traffic patterns

## Confidence

- **High confidence**: The claim that BjTT is the largest publicly available multimodal traffic dataset (based on clear quantitative comparisons with existing datasets)
- **Medium confidence**: The effectiveness of text-guided generative models for traffic prediction (supported by experimental results but limited by evaluation methodology)
- **Low confidence**: The generalizability of findings beyond Beijing's traffic patterns due to the limited geographic and temporal scope
