# Diffusion-based Graph Generative Methods

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2401.15617
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.15617
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Comprehensive survey of diffusion-based graph generative methods focusing on molecular design and motion synthesis

## Executive Summary
This survey provides a systematic overview of diffusion-based graph generative methods, categorizing them into three main paradigms: DDPMs, SGMs, and Score SDEs. The paper examines how these approaches are adapted for discrete graph data and their applications in molecule generation, 3D structure design, ligand docking, and human motion synthesis. The survey identifies key challenges including discrete graph structures, out-of-distribution generation, and evaluation metrics, while highlighting future directions such as integration with AIGC and supervised learning tasks.

## Method Summary
The survey systematically categorizes diffusion-based graph generative methods into three paradigms: denoising diffusion probabilistic models (DDPMs), score-based generative models (SGMs), and score-based stochastic differential equations (Score SDEs). Each paradigm is analyzed for its adaptation to discrete graph structures, with specific focus on applications in molecular generation and motion synthesis. The paper discusses how these models handle the unique challenges of graph data, including node ordering invariance and edge prediction in molecular structures. Common datasets and evaluation metrics are identified, including ZINC for molecules and Human3.6M for motion data, with metrics such as RMSD, FID, and validity scores.

## Key Results
- Diffusion models show strong performance in 3D molecule design and conformation generation
- Three main paradigms (DDPMs, SGMs, Score SDEs) have been successfully adapted for discrete graph data
- Current challenges include discrete graph structures, out-of-distribution generation, and evaluation metric standardization

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
Diffusion models work effectively for graph generation because they learn to gradually denoise corrupted graph structures through a reverse process. The models are trained to predict the original graph structure from progressively noised versions, which allows them to capture complex dependencies in molecular structures and human motion patterns. The probabilistic nature of diffusion models enables them to generate diverse and realistic graph samples while maintaining structural validity.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map:** Diffusion Process -> Graph Encoder -> Noising Schedule -> Score Network -> Graph Decoder

**Critical Path:** Input graph → Graph Encoder → Noising Schedule → Score Network → Graph Decoder → Generated graph

**Design Tradeoffs:**
- Discrete vs. continuous representations of graph structures
- Complexity of noising schedules vs. generation quality
- Computational efficiency vs. sample diversity
- Domain-specific constraints vs. general applicability

**Failure Signatures:**
- Invalid molecular structures with incorrect bond valence
- Disconnected graph components in generated molecules
- Unrealistic human motion patterns with physical impossibilities
- Mode collapse resulting in limited diversity of generated samples
