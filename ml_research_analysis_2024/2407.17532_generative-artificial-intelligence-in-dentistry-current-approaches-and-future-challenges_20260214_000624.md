# Generative artificial intelligence in dentistry: Current approaches and future challenges

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2407.17532
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.17532

## Executive Summary
Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) is rapidly transforming dentistry by enabling personalized education, enhancing clinical practice, and accelerating research. GenAI models, such as text and image generators, provide innovative tools for dental students, practitioners, and researchers. In education, GenAI supports personalized learning, assessment, and feedback, while in clinical practice, it aids in diagnosis, treatment planning, and communication. In research, GenAI facilitates scientific writing, drug discovery, and data analysis. However, challenges such as resistance to change, ethical considerations, and the need for AI literacy must be addressed to ensure effective and ethical adoption of GenAI in dentistry.

## Method Summary
The paper provides a comprehensive review of generative AI applications in dentistry, discussing current approaches and future challenges. It describes various GenAI techniques, including generative adversarial networks (GANs), diffusion models, and transformers, and explores their potential applications in dental education, clinical practice, and research. The paper also addresses ethical considerations and challenges associated with implementing GenAI in dentistry.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: GenAI models bridge the usability gap in AI by providing natural language interfaces, making complex models accessible to non-experts.
- Mechanism: Natural language processing allows users to interact with AI systems without needing technical expertise, democratizing access to AI capabilities.
- Core assumption: The natural language interface effectively translates user intent into actionable AI queries and outputs.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "GenAI models bridge the usability gap of AI by providing a natural language interface to interact with complex models."
  - [section] "Contemporary AI is perceived as a conversational technology that enables humans to engage in straightforward discussions and receive relevant responses."
- Break condition: If the natural language interface fails to accurately interpret user intent or generate appropriate responses, the usability bridge collapses.

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: Large language models exhibit emergent abilities when scaled up, enabling them to perform tasks they weren't explicitly trained for.
- Mechanism: Increasing model size and training data allows the model to develop new capabilities through pattern recognition and generalization.
- Core assumption: The emergent abilities are consistent and reliable across different tasks and contexts.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [section] "Research has shown that increasing the size of causal language models leads to improved performance... These emergent abilities include in-context learning, instruction following, and step-by-step reasoning."
- Break condition: If emergent abilities prove unreliable or inconsistent across different use cases, the scaling hypothesis breaks down.

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: Diffusion-based image generators offer fine-grained control over image generation through natural language descriptions.
- Mechanism: Transformer-based text encoders convert natural language descriptions into numerical representations that guide the diffusion process.
- Core assumption: The numerical representations accurately capture the semantic meaning of the natural language descriptions.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [section] "Diffusion-based image generators use transformer-based text encoders to represent the input natural language description and then decode this representation as an image through diffusion."
- Break condition: If the text-to-image mapping becomes too lossy or the generated images consistently fail to match the descriptions, the control mechanism fails.
