# GeneOH Diffusion: Towards Generalizable Hand-Object Interaction Denoising via Denoising Diffusion

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2402.14810
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.14810
- **Authors**: Xueyi Liu; Li Yi
- **Reference count**: 40
- **Primary result**: GeneOH Diffusion achieves state-of-the-art denoising performance across four benchmarks, demonstrating superior generalization to new interactions and diverse noise patterns.

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces GeneOH Diffusion, a novel method for denoising hand-object interaction sequences. The key innovation is GeneOH, a contact-centric representation that parameterizes the interaction process by encoding hand trajectories, spatial relations, and temporal relations. To address generalization challenges, the authors propose a "denoising via diffusion" strategy, where a canonical denoiser projects noisy data from a whitened noise space to a clean data manifold. This approach is complemented by a progressive denoising strategy that refines the input through three stages, each focusing on a specific component of GeneOH. Extensive experiments on four benchmarks demonstrate the superior effectiveness of GeneOH Diffusion, showcasing its ability to generalize to new interactions and diverse noise patterns while producing high-quality results. The method also shows promise for various downstream applications, such as cleaning synthesized motions and refining motion-retargeted trajectories.

## Method Summary
GeneOH Diffusion addresses the challenge of denoising hand-object interaction sequences by introducing a contact-centric representation (GeneOH) and a novel "denoising via diffusion" strategy. GeneOH parameterizes the interaction process using generalized contact points, canonicalized hand trajectories relative to these points, and temporal relations (velocities and distances). The denoising strategy involves learning a canonical denoising model that maps noisy data from a whitened noise space to a clean data manifold, combined with a progressive denoising approach that refines the input through three stages: motion denoising, spatial relation denoising, and temporal relation denoising. This method is trained on datasets with known noise patterns and evaluated on multiple benchmarks, demonstrating superior performance in handling various noise types and generalizing to new interactions.

## Key Results
- GeneOH Diffusion outperforms state-of-the-art methods on four benchmarks (GRAB, HOI4D, ARCTIC, HON4D) in terms of MPJPE, penetration depth, and contact IoU metrics.
- The method generalizes effectively to unseen noise patterns, including real-world depth sensing and vision estimation errors, as well as synthetic Beta-distributed noise.
- GeneOH Diffusion shows promising results in downstream applications such as cleaning synthesized motions and refining motion-retargeted trajectories.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- **Claim**: The GeneOH representation achieves better generalization by canonicalizing hand trajectories and spatial relations relative to contact-centric object points.
- **Mechanism**: GeneOH encodes hand-object interactions using generalized contact points (surface samples near hand trajectory), canonicalized hand trajectories (relative to contact point poses), and temporal relations (velocities and distances). This reduces inter-sequence variance by aligning interactions in a coordinate frame induced by the interaction region.
- **Core assumption**: Contact points near the hand trajectory capture the essence of the interaction, and canonicalization using these points removes irrelevant object pose variations.
- **Evidence anchors**:
  - [abstract] "GeneOH informatively parameterizes the HOI process, facilitating enhanced generalization across various HOI scenarios."
  - [section 3.1] "GeneOH adopts a contact-centric perspective and incorporates an innovative canonicalization strategy. This approach effectively reduces disparities between different sequences, promoting generalization across diverse HOI scenarios."
  - [corpus] No direct corpus evidence; weak support from related works.
- **Break condition**: If contact points do not align with the actual manipulation area, canonicalization could mislead the model.

### Mechanism 2
- **Claim**: The "denoising via diffusion" strategy enables the model to handle out-of-distribution noise by projecting noisy inputs into a whitened noise space before denoising.
- **Mechanism**: The method first diffuses the input noisy trajectory with Gaussian noise to align it with the whitened noise space used during training, then applies a canonical denoiser trained to map samples from this space to the clean data manifold.
- **Core assumption**: The whitened noise space, constructed by diffusing clean data, captures the essential noise structure needed to generalize across different noise patterns.
- **Evidence anchors**:
  - [abstract] "The new denoising scheme consists of a canonical denoising model trained to project noisy data samples from a whitened noise space to a clean data manifold and a 'denoising via diffusion' strategy which can handle input trajectories with various noise patterns."
  - [section 3.2] "We propose to learn a canonical denoising model that describes the mapping from a whitened noise space to the data manifold. The whitened noise space contains noisy data diffused from clean data in the training dataset via Gaussian noise at various noise scales."
  - [corpus] No direct corpus evidence; mechanism is novel.
- **Break condition**: If the diffusion step adds too much noise, the input may drift too far from the original signal to be recoverable.

### Mechanism 3
- **Claim**: Progressive denoising across three stages (motion, spatial, temporal) allows the model to handle complex, heterogeneous noise more effectively than single-stage denoising.
- **Mechanism**: The model first denoises the canonicalized hand trajectory, then hand-object spatial relations, and finally temporal relations. Each stage preserves the natural structure cleaned in previous stages, ensuring consistent refinement.
- **Core assumption**: The three GeneOH components (¯J, S, T) are sufficiently independent to be denoised separately without losing information or causing inconsistency.
- **Evidence anchors**:
  - [abstract] "Instead of learning to clean the interaction noise through a single stage, we devise a progressive denoising strategy where the input is sequentially refined via three stages, each of which concentrates on cleaning one specific component of GeneOH."
  - [section 3.2] "Based on the domain-generalizable denoising strategy, designing a single data-driven model to clean heterogeneous interaction noise in one stage is still not feasible. The interaction noise contains various kinds of noise at ununiform scales stemming from different reasons."
  - [corpus] No direct corpus evidence; progressive refinement is a known principle but application here is novel.
- **Break condition**: If noise in one component strongly correlates with another, separating stages may fail to remove it effectively.

## Foundational Learning

- **Concept**: Diffusion probabilistic models
  - **Why needed here**: The method leverages diffusion-based denoising, so understanding how forward diffusion adds noise and how reverse diffusion removes it is essential.
  - **Quick check question**: What does the variance schedule control in a diffusion process?

- **Concept**: Canonicalization and coordinate system alignment
  - **Why needed here**: GeneOH canonicalizes trajectories relative to object contact points to reduce inter-sequence variance; understanding this requires knowledge of coordinate transformations.
  - **Quick check question**: How does expressing hand positions relative to object contact points help with generalization?

- **Concept**: Temporal coherence in sequences
  - **Why needed here**: The temporal denoising stage enforces consistent hand-object motion; understanding velocity-based constraints is critical.
  - **Quick check question**: Why might relative velocities between hand and object points indicate temporal artifacts?

## Architecture Onboarding

- **Component map**: Noisy input -> GeneOH encoding -> MotionDiff -> SpatialDiff -> TemporalDiff -> final denoised trajectory
- **Critical path**: Noisy input → GeneOH encoding → MotionDiff → SpatialDiff → TemporalDiff → final denoised trajectory
- **Design tradeoffs**:
  - Progressive vs. single-stage denoising: More stages reduce error propagation but increase inference time.
  - Contact-centric vs. global canonicalization: Contact-centric may better capture interaction-relevant structure but risks missing global pose cues.
- **Failure signatures**:
  - Large penetrations persisting after denoising → SpatialDiff stage insufficient
  - Unnatural hand motion or jitter → TemporalDiff stage insufficient
  - Excessive deviation from input → Diffusion timestep too large
- **First 3 experiments**:
  1. Run MotionDiff only on a simple synthetic noisy sequence; check if canonicalized trajectory improves over baseline.
  2. Apply full 3-stage pipeline on GRAB test set with Gaussian noise; measure MPJPE and penetration metrics.
  3. Test generalization by running on HOI4D test set; compare IV and penetration depth to TOCH baseline.

## Limitations
- **Contact point sensitivity**: The effectiveness of GeneOH canonicalization depends on accurate contact point detection, which may fail in ambiguous interaction regions.
- **Object pose assumption**: GeneOH Diffusion assumes accurate object pose trajectories, limiting its applicability to real-world scenarios where object poses are noisy or estimated.
- **Noise distribution sensitivity**: The method's generalization ability may be sensitive to the choice of noise distribution used during training, as the diffusion strategy relies on aligning noisy inputs with the whitened noise space.

## Confidence

**High confidence** in the method's novel combination of contact-centric representation and progressive diffusion-based denoising as a theoretically sound approach to HOI denoising.

**Medium confidence** in the practical effectiveness of GeneOH canonicalization for generalization across diverse HOI scenarios.

**Medium confidence** in the denoising via diffusion strategy's ability to handle out-of-distribution noise patterns.

**Low confidence** in the assumption that progressive denoising across three stages will consistently outperform single-stage approaches for all noise types.

## Next Checks

1. **Ablation on contact point detection sensitivity**: Systematically vary the contact point detection parameters (e.g., distance thresholds, surface sampling density) and measure the impact on downstream denoising performance.

2. **Cross-dataset noise transfer test**: Train the denoising models on GRAB, then evaluate on ARCTIC with synthetic noise distributions that differ significantly from those seen during training (e.g., non-Gaussian, heteroscedastic noise).

3. **Component correlation analysis**: Measure the statistical correlation between motion, spatial, and temporal noise components in the training data. If strong correlations exist, this would challenge the progressive denoising assumption.
