# Enhancing Kurdish Text-to-Speech with Native Corpus Training: A High-Quality WaveGlow Vocoder Approach

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2409.13734
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.13734
- Reference count: 6
- Primary result: Achieved MOS 4.91 on Kurdish speech synthesis using native WaveGlow vocoder

## Executive Summary
This study addresses the challenge of developing high-quality text-to-speech (TTS) systems for low-resource languages, specifically Kurdish, by training a WaveGlow vocoder on a 21-hour Kurdish speech corpus. The approach involves adapting the WaveGlow deep learning architecture to Kurdish's unique acoustic properties and implementing advanced prosody modeling techniques. The model was trained from scratch on the Sabat Speech Corpus, achieving a Mean Opinion Score (MOS) of 4.91, which sets a new benchmark for Kurdish speech synthesis. This result significantly outperforms models using English pre-trained vocoders, demonstrating the effectiveness of language-specific TTS model development for low-resource languages.

## Method Summary
The researchers developed a Kurdish text-to-speech system by training a WaveGlow vocoder from scratch on a 21-hour Kurdish speech corpus. The approach involved adapting the WaveGlow deep learning architecture to handle Kurdish's specific acoustic characteristics and implementing prosody modeling techniques suitable for the language. The model was trained on the Sabat Speech Corpus, with careful attention to capturing the unique phonetic and prosodic features of Kurdish. The training process emphasized native language modeling rather than transfer learning from English pre-trained models.

## Key Results
- Achieved Mean Opinion Score (MOS) of 4.91 for Kurdish speech synthesis
- Outperformed models using English pre-trained vocoders
- Set new benchmark for Kurdish speech synthesis quality

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The superior performance of the native WaveGlow vocoder stems from its ability to learn the specific acoustic and prosodic patterns of Kurdish from scratch, rather than attempting to adapt English speech patterns. By training directly on Kurdish data, the model captures the language's unique phonetic characteristics, intonation patterns, and rhythm without the interference of English speech patterns. This native training approach allows the model to develop more accurate representations of Kurdish speech sounds and their temporal relationships, resulting in more natural-sounding synthesis.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Text Processing -> Mel-Spectrogram Generation -> WaveGlow Vocoder -> Raw Audio Output

**Critical Path**: The core pipeline involves text normalization and linguistic feature extraction, followed by mel-spectrogram generation, which serves as input to the WaveGlow vocoder that produces the final audio waveform.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The choice to train from scratch on Kurdish data rather than using transfer learning from English models prioritizes accuracy for Kurdish-specific features but requires more training data and computational resources. The 21-hour corpus size represents a balance between model quality and the practical limitations of low-resource language data availability.

**Failure Signatures**: Common failure modes include mispronunciation of Kurdish-specific phonemes, unnatural prosody when handling complex sentences, and degradation in audio quality for rare words or names not well-represented in the training corpus.

**First Experiments**: 1) Evaluate model performance on diverse Kurdish dialects to assess generalization; 2) Test model robustness with varying text complexity and domain-specific vocabulary; 3) Compare synthesized speech quality across different recording conditions and speaker characteristics.

## Limitations
- 21-hour training corpus, while substantial for a low-resource language, remains relatively small compared to large-scale TTS datasets
- Evaluation methodology lacks detailed information about rater selection criteria and inter-rater reliability measures
- No quantitative comparisons against baseline systems beyond English pre-trained vocoders

## Confidence
- **High confidence**: The achievement of MOS 4.91 with the native WaveGlow vocoder is directly measurable and represents the primary experimental outcome
- **Medium confidence**: The superiority over English pre-trained vocoders is demonstrated but lacks comprehensive ablation studies or multiple baseline comparisons
- **Medium confidence**: The claim of setting a new benchmark for Kurdish speech synthesis is valid within the study's scope but lacks broader context about existing Kurdish TTS systems
