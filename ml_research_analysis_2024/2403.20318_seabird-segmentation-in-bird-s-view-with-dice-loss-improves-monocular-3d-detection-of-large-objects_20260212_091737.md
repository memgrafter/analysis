# SeaBird: Segmentation in Bird's View with Dice Loss Improves Monocular 3D Detection of Large Objects

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2403.20318
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.20318

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of monocular 3D object detection (Mono3D) generalization to large objects, which is crucial for autonomous vehicles. The authors identify the sensitivity of depth regression losses to noise in large objects as a key cause of failure. To overcome this, they propose SeaBird, a method that combines Bird's Eye View (BEV) segmentation with dice loss for Mono3D. Theoretical analysis demonstrates that dice loss is more noise-robust than regression losses for large objects. SeaBird achieves state-of-the-art performance on the KITTI-360 and nuScenes leaderboards, particularly excelling in detecting large objects.

## Method Summary
SeaBird addresses the problem of monocular 3D object detection (Mono3D) generalization to large objects by integrating Bird's Eye View (BEV) segmentation with dice loss. The authors argue that the sensitivity of depth regression losses to noise in large objects is a key cause of failure. SeaBird's architecture leverages BEV segmentation to generate object proposals and utilizes dice loss for training, which is theoretically shown to be more noise-robust than traditional regression losses for large objects. This approach improves the detection of large objects in monocular 3D detection tasks, achieving state-of-the-art results on the KITTI-360 and nuScenes leaderboards.

## Architecture Onboarding

Component Map:
- Input Image -> BEV Segmentation -> Object Proposals -> Dice Loss Training -> Monocular 3D Detection

Critical Path:
The critical path in SeaBird involves the integration of BEV segmentation and dice loss training. The BEV segmentation generates object proposals from the input image, which are then used to train the model with dice loss. This approach improves the robustness of the model, particularly for large objects, leading to better monocular 3D detection performance.

Design Tradeoffs:
- BEV Segmentation vs. Direct Regression: BEV segmentation provides a more structured approach to object detection, but may introduce additional computational overhead compared to direct regression methods.
- Dice Loss vs. Regression Loss: Dice loss offers improved noise robustness for large objects but may require careful tuning to balance with other loss components.

Failure Signatures:
- Poor performance on small objects due to the focus on large object detection.
- Potential overfitting to specific datasets if not properly regularized.

## Limitations
- The theoretical noise-robustness analysis of dice loss versus regression losses assumes uniform noise distribution and may not fully capture complex noise patterns in real-world scenarios.
- The ablation studies focus primarily on dice loss and BEV segmentation integration, without extensively exploring alternative segmentation architectures or loss functions.
- The claim of state-of-the-art performance is based on comparisons with published results, and the exact evaluation protocols and data splits may vary across methods.
