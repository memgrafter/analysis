# Accelerating Medical Knowledge Discovery through Automated Knowledge Graph Generation and Enrichment

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2405.02321
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.02321
- Reference count: 8
- This paper presents M-KGA, an automated medical knowledge graph generation framework that addresses gaps in connectivity within knowledge graphs

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces M-KGA, an automated framework for medical knowledge graph generation that leverages user-provided medical concepts, BioPortal ontologies for semantic enrichment, and Clinical BERT embeddings to discover hidden connections between medical concepts. The framework employs both cluster-based and node-based methodologies to uncover relationships, with the node-based approach demonstrating superior performance. The system successfully processes both structured and unstructured medical data in real-time, offering a comprehensive solution for medical knowledge graph automation.

## Method Summary
M-KGA operates by taking user-provided medical concepts as input and utilizing BioPortal ontologies to semantically enrich these concepts. The framework employs Clinical BERT embeddings to discover hidden connections between medical concepts, implementing both cluster-based and node-based methodologies for relationship discovery. The approach is designed to handle both structured and unstructured medical data, processing information in real-time to construct comprehensive knowledge graphs that can be applied to medical diagnosis, coding, and claim verification.

## Key Results
- Node-based method achieved superior performance compared to cluster-based approach
- All metrics (accuracy, F1 score, recall, precision) showed node-based method outperforming cluster-based approach
- Framework successfully processed both structured and unstructured medical data in real-time

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework's effectiveness stems from its multi-layered approach to knowledge graph construction. By combining user-provided medical concepts with BioPortal ontologies for semantic enrichment, the system creates a robust foundation for knowledge representation. The use of Clinical BERT embeddings enables the discovery of complex relationships between medical concepts that might not be apparent through traditional methods. The dual methodology (cluster-based and node-based) allows for comprehensive relationship mapping, with the node-based approach proving more effective at identifying precise connections between individual medical concepts.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: User Input -> BioPortal Ontology Enrichment -> Clinical BERT Embedding -> Relationship Discovery (Node-based/Cluster-based) -> Knowledge Graph Output

**Critical Path**: The critical path involves receiving user-provided medical concepts, enriching them through BioPortal ontologies, generating Clinical BERT embeddings, and then applying the node-based methodology for relationship discovery to produce the final knowledge graph.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The framework trades computational complexity for comprehensiveness by using both node-based and cluster-based methodologies. The choice of Clinical BERT embeddings over other embedding techniques prioritizes medical domain specificity over general language understanding.

**Failure Signatures**: Potential failures include: insufficient concept coverage from BioPortal ontologies, poor embedding quality leading to incorrect relationship discovery, scalability issues with larger datasets, and ambiguous concept mapping resulting in false connections.
