# SAR-AE-SFP: SAR Imagery Adversarial Example in Real Physics domain with Target Scattering Feature Parameters

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2403.01210
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.01210

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces SAR-AE-SFP-Attack, a method for generating real physics adversarial examples in Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) imagery by perturbing the scattering feature parameters of 3D target objects. The approach integrates SAR imaging mechanisms using the RaySAR physical simulator to adjust reflection and scattering coefficients, thereby altering echo intensity and producing adversarial examples after imaging processing. By optimizing coherent energy accumulation through finite difference methods and batch processing techniques, the method significantly outperforms traditional digital adversarial attack methods on both CNN-based and Transformer-based models.

## Method Summary
The SAR-AE-SFP-Attack method generates real physics adversarial examples by directly modifying the scattering feature parameters (reflection and scattering coefficients) of 3D target models. These perturbations are processed through the RaySAR physical simulator, which models electromagnetic scattering to calculate echo intensities. The method employs finite difference gradient estimation to optimize these parameters efficiently, even in the non-differentiable simulation environment. Batch processing techniques are used to improve optimization efficiency. The approach is evaluated using the MSTAR database and simulated datasets with specific elevation and azimuth angles, demonstrating significant improvements in attack success rates compared to digital adversarial methods.

## Key Results
- Attack success rate improved by over 30% on CNN-based models compared to digital adversarial attacks
- Attack success rate improved by over 13% on Transformer-based models compared to digital adversarial attacks
- Strong transferability demonstrated across different models and viewing perspectives

### Component Map
3D Target Model -> Scattering Feature Parameters -> RaySAR Physical Simulator -> SAR Image -> Classification Model

### Critical Path
The critical path involves perturbing scattering feature parameters, simulating the resulting SAR image through RaySAR, and optimizing these perturbations to maximize classification error while minimizing parameter changes.

### Design Tradeoffs
- Physics-based approach vs. digital pixel modification: Provides real-world applicability but requires complex physical simulation
- Finite difference gradient estimation vs. analytical gradients: Enables optimization in non-differentiable environments but may be computationally intensive

### Failure Signatures
- Poor attack effectiveness may indicate suboptimal parameter perturbation strategies or insufficient optimization iterations
- Inaccurate gradient estimation could result from too large or too small perturbation sizes in the finite difference method

## Limitations
- The dependency on the RaySAR physical simulator introduces potential uncertainties in how well the simulator captures real-world electromagnetic scattering phenomena
- The finite difference method for gradient estimation, while practical, may be computationally intensive and could potentially miss optimal perturbations due to its discrete nature
- The paper doesn't extensively explore the robustness of the generated adversarial examples under different environmental conditions or sensor configurations
