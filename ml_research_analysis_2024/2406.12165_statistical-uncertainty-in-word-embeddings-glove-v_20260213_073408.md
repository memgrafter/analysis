# Statistical Uncertainty in Word Embeddings: GloVe-V

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2406.12165
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.12165
- Authors: Andrea Vallebueno; Cassandra Handan-Nader; Christopher D. Manning; Daniel E. Ho
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Introduces GloVe-V, a method to quantify statistical uncertainty in GloVe word embeddings by approximating reconstruction error variances

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces GloVe-V, a method to quantify statistical uncertainty in GloVe word embeddings by approximating reconstruction error variances. The authors reformulate GloVe as a probabilistic model, enabling tractable computation of word-specific covariance matrices under conditional independence assumptions. GloVe-V allows principled hypothesis testing in downstream tasks such as word similarity, model comparison, and bias measurement. Experiments on the COHA corpus show that uncertainty decreases with word frequency and that ignoring it can lead to incorrect conclusions about model performance and bias.

## Method Summary
GloVe-V reformulates the GloVe embedding model as a probabilistic framework by treating each word's co-occurrence vector as a weighted multivariate normal distribution. Under conditional independence assumptions, the method computes word-specific covariance matrices that approximate the reconstruction error variances. These covariance matrices can be efficiently propagated to downstream test statistics using the delta method or Monte Carlo sampling, enabling statistically rigorous hypothesis testing.

## Key Results
- GloVe-V variances decrease with word frequency and provide more meaningful uncertainty estimates than document bootstrap methods
- Incorporating uncertainty can change conclusions about textual similarities, model selection, and textual bias
- The approach is computationally efficient, scalable, and provides approximately calibrated intervals

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: GloVe-V enables principled hypothesis testing by quantifying statistical uncertainty in word embedding locations.
- Mechanism: By reformulating GloVe as a probabilistic model under conditional independence assumptions, GloVe-V computes word-specific covariance matrices that approximate reconstruction error variances.
- Core assumption: Conditional independence of word co-occurrence rows given optimal context vectors and constant terms.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "enabling principled hypothesis testing in core word embedding tasks"
  - [section] "we write the following weighted multivariate normal model for the rows of X"
- Break condition: If the conditional independence assumption is violated (e.g., highly correlated co-occurrence patterns), the variance estimates become unreliable.

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: GloVe-V provides more meaningful uncertainty estimates than document bootstrap methods.
- Mechanism: GloVe-V captures uncertainty due to sparsity in the underlying co-occurrence matrix, while document bootstrap captures uncertainty from document selection.
- Core assumption: The corpus itself is fixed and the uncertainty comes from the data-generating process of word co-occurrences.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "they address uncertainty from document or lexicon selection, even though embeddings are parameters of a data generating process on word co-occurrences"
  - [section] "document-level uncertainty can either underestimate or overestimate the variance of a downstream test statistic compared to the reconstruction error uncertainty given by GloVe-V"
- Break condition: If the researcher's goal is to assess sensitivity to document selection rather than reconstruction error, document bootstrap may be more appropriate.

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: GloVe-V variances can be efficiently propagated to downstream tasks using the delta method.
- Mechanism: For differentiable test statistics, the delta method provides an efficient way to compute asymptotic variances by using a first-order Taylor series approximation.
- Core assumption: The test statistic is differentiable with respect to the word embeddings.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "we show that incorporating uncertainty can change conclusions about textual similarities, model selection, and textual bias"
  - [section] "Using a first-order Taylor series approximation to the test statistic, the delta method states that if √n(W − ˆW) converges to N (0, Σ), then √n(ϕ(W) − ϕ( ˆW)) converges to N (0, ϕ′(W)TΣϕ′(W))"
- Break condition: If the test statistic is not differentiable, the delta method cannot be used and Monte Carlo sampling from the multivariate normal distribution must be employed instead.

## Architecture Onboarding

- Component map: GloVe model -> Probabilistic reformulation -> Covariance matrix computation -> Uncertainty propagation (delta method or Monte Carlo sampling)
- Critical path: 1) Train GloVe model, 2) Compute GloVe-V covariance matrices, 3) Propagate uncertainties to downstream tasks
- Design tradeoffs: GloVe-V trades off some statistical rigor (due to conditional independence assumption) for computational tractability on large vocabularies
- Failure signatures: GloVe-V variances may be unreliable if the conditional independence assumption is violated, if the number of context words is too small relative to the embedding dimensionality, or if the test statistic is not differentiable
- First 3 experiments:
  1. Compute GloVe-V variances for a small vocabulary and visualize the uncertainty ellipses
  2. Compare GloVe-V uncertainties to document bootstrap uncertainties for a downstream test statistic
  3. Use the delta method to propagate GloVe-V uncertainties to a cosine similarity test statistic

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out

### Open Question 1
- Question: How would incorporating uncertainty in context vectors and bias terms (currently treated as constants) affect the covariance estimates and downstream inference?
- Basis in paper: [explicit] The authors note that treating context vectors and bias terms as constants is a computational necessity but acknowledge this is only one source of uncertainty.
- Why unresolved: The paper uses conditional independence assumptions to make the variance computation tractable, but doesn't explore how relaxing this assumption would change results.
- What evidence would resolve it: A comparative analysis showing GloVe-V results with and without uncertainty in context vectors/bias terms on the same downstream tasks.

### Open Question 2
- Question: Would the GloVe-V approach generalize to other word embedding models like word2vec or BERT, and how would the variance estimation differ?
- Basis in paper: [inferred] The authors state the methodology applies solely to GloVe due to its statistical foundations, but suggest similar uncertainty questions apply to transformer models.
- Why unresolved: The probabilistic reformulation that enables GloVe-V's tractable variance computation is specific to GloVe's objective function.
- What evidence would resolve it: A demonstration of equivalent variance estimation methods for word2vec and BERT embeddings, including validation of their statistical properties.

### Open Question 3
- Question: How does GloVe-V uncertainty interact with time-varying bias estimates when analyzing corpora across multiple decades?
- Basis in paper: [explicit] The authors show GloVe-V can reveal when time trends in bias scores are not statistically significant, but don't systematically study how uncertainty changes over time.
- Why unresolved: The paper only provides a single example of time-varying bias analysis without exploring whether uncertainty patterns themselves evolve.
- What evidence would resolve it: A comprehensive analysis of how GloVe-V intervals change across decades for multiple bias metrics, including whether certain time periods show systematically higher uncertainty.

## Limitations
- The conditional independence assumption may be violated in practice, leading to unreliable variance estimates
- The computational cost of computing and storing full covariance matrices grows quadratically with dimensionality
- The framework is specifically tailored to GloVe and may not generalize to other embedding methods

## Confidence
- **High Confidence:** The computational efficiency and scalability of GloVe-V compared to document bootstrap methods
- **Medium Confidence:** The accuracy of variance estimates under the conditional independence assumption and the practical impact of uncertainty quantification on downstream tasks
- **Low Confidence:** The applicability of GloVe-V to other embedding methods beyond GloVe and related matrix factorization approaches
