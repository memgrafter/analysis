# Resilience in Knowledge Graph Embeddings

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2410.21163
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.21163
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Survey identifies gaps in resilience research for knowledge graph embeddings, with most work focusing only on robustness aspects

## Executive Summary
This paper provides a comprehensive survey of resilience in knowledge graph embedding (KGE) models, defining resilience across five distinct aspects: generalization consistency, distribution adaptation, performance consistency, robustness, and stability to incomplete input. The authors systematically categorize existing approaches into optimization-based, gradient-based, GAN-based, reinforcement learning, multi-modal, and ensemble methods. The survey reveals a significant research gap, with most literature focusing exclusively on adversarial and non-adversarial robustness while largely neglecting generalization consistency and distribution adaptation. The paper identifies confidence-aware and multi-modal approaches as particularly effective for improving robustness, while highlighting the need for comprehensive models that address all aspects of resilience.

## Method Summary
The authors conducted a systematic literature review to identify and categorize research on resilience in knowledge graph embeddings. They defined five key aspects of resilience and analyzed existing works through these lenses, organizing approaches into six methodological categories. The survey examined the effectiveness of different techniques, particularly focusing on confidence-aware and multi-modal methods for robustness improvement. The analysis also identified research gaps and proposed future directions for developing comprehensive resilience models that address all five aspects of resilience.

## Key Results
- Most KGE resilience research focuses only on adversarial and non-adversarial robustness, neglecting generalization consistency and distribution adaptation
- Confidence-aware and multi-modal approaches show particular effectiveness in improving robustness
- Significant research gaps exist in developing models that address all five aspects of resilience simultaneously
- Optimization-based, gradient-based, GAN-based, reinforcement learning, multi-modal, and ensemble methods are the primary categories of resilience approaches
