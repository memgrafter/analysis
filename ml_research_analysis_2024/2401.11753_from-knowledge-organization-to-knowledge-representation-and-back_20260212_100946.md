# From Knowledge Organization to Knowledge Representation and Back

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2401.11753
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.11753
- Reference count: 3
- Authors bridge KO and KR traditions to enable scalable, principled knowledge organization across any domain

## Executive Summary
This paper bridges two distinct traditions in knowledge modeling: Knowledge Organization (KO), rooted in facet-analytical methods for bibliographic cataloguing, and Knowledge Representation (KR), which uses computational models like ontologies and knowledge graphs to represent any domain entity. While KO offers rigorous guiding principles but lacks technological scalability, KR excels in technology-driven applications but lacks methodological structure. The authors perform a functional mapping between KO and KR methodologies and propose an integrated KR-enriched KO methodology that combines the guiding principles of KO with the technological capabilities of KR. This integration expands the scope of KO from bibliographic resources to any real-world entity and enables diverse, purpose-specific services.

## Method Summary
The authors conduct a systematic comparison between KO and KR methodologies, identifying their respective strengths and limitations. They perform a functional mapping between the four phases of KO (identify universe of discourse, create entity type graph, identify canons, and design presentation structure) and corresponding KR phases (requirements analysis, ontology engineering, knowledge graph creation, and deployment). Based on this mapping, they propose a KR-enriched KO methodology that maintains KO's guiding principles while leveraging KR's technological capabilities. The methodology is implemented in the Digital University of the University of Trento, where it supports institutional portals, research dashboards, open data publication, and student mobile applications.

## Key Results
- Successfully mapped KO and KR methodologies across four functional phases
- Developed KR-enriched KO methodology combining KO principles with KR technology
- Demonstrated implementation in Digital University supporting multiple services including portals, dashboards, and mobile apps
- Showed methodology's scalability through practical deployment in institutional setting

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The integration works because it preserves KO's rigorous methodological framework while incorporating KR's technological capabilities. KO provides systematic principles for organizing knowledge through facet analysis and canon-compliant structuring, while KR offers computational models and tools for implementation. By mapping these approaches functionally, the methodology maintains KO's guiding principles for knowledge organization while enabling scalable technological deployment through KR's infrastructure. The Ontology Engineer role ensures canon compliance in the KR context, maintaining quality while leveraging modern technology.
