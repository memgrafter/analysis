# Voxel Scene Graph for Intracranial Hemorrhage

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2407.21580
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.21580
- Reference count: 23
- Achieves up to 74% recall of clinically relevant relationships between ICH and brain structures

## Executive Summary
This paper presents a novel approach for detecting and understanding complex relationships between intracranial hemorrhage and adjacent brain structures in CT scans. The authors develop a two-stage method combining object detection with scene graph generation (SGG) techniques adapted for 3D voxel data. The system identifies bleeding, ventricle system, and midline structures, then predicts their spatial relationships. Tested on two head-CT datasets, the method demonstrates significant improvements over state-of-the-art object detection approaches and shows promise for clinical applications in patient outcome prediction and treatment planning.

## Method Summary
The proposed approach employs a two-stage architecture: first, a hybrid Retina-UNet model performs object detection to identify key anatomical structures (bleeding, ventricles, midline) in 3D CT scans; second, voxel-adapted versions of Neural Motifs and Iterative Message Passing algorithms generate scene graphs predicting relationships between these structures. The system processes volumetric data directly, maintaining spatial context throughout both stages. The scene graph generation specifically focuses on clinically relevant relationships such as proximity and displacement patterns that are critical for assessing hemorrhage severity and potential complications.

## Key Results
- Achieves up to 74% recall of clinically relevant relationships between ICH and brain structures
- Outperforms state-of-the-art object detection methods on intracranial hemorrhage datasets
- Demonstrates effectiveness on both public (10 cases) and proprietary (12 cases) head-CT datasets

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method leverages the complementary strengths of object detection for precise anatomical localization and scene graph generation for capturing complex spatial relationships. By adapting SGG techniques from 2D computer vision to 3D voxel data, the system can model clinically meaningful patterns such as ventricular compression and midline shift that are critical for ICH assessment. The hybrid Retina-UNet architecture effectively combines the localization accuracy of RetinaNet with the semantic understanding of UNet, enabling robust detection of both hemorrhage and anatomical structures in challenging CT imaging conditions.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: CT Scan -> Hybrid Retina-UNet -> Object Detection -> Scene Graph Generation -> Relationship Prediction

**Critical Path**: The bottleneck lies in the scene graph generation stage, where computational complexity scales with the number of detected objects and message passing iterations. The system must balance relationship prediction accuracy against computational efficiency.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The approach trades computational complexity for improved relationship modeling capabilities. While more complex than simple detection, the scene graph generation captures clinically relevant patterns that pure detection would miss. The voxel-based approach maintains spatial fidelity but requires more computational resources than slice-based methods.

**Failure Signatures**: 
- False negatives in object detection cascade to missing relationships
- Incorrect anatomical localization leads to spurious relationship predictions
- Message passing instability can produce inconsistent relationship assignments
- Limited training data may cause overfitting to specific hemorrhage patterns

3. Test system performance on cases with varying hemorrhage sizes and locations to assess robustness

## Limitations
- Performance evaluated on small datasets (10-12 cases), raising generalizability concerns
- 74% recall leaves 26% of critical relationships undetected, potentially impacting clinical decisions
- Limited to binary relationships between three structure types, missing complex pathology interactions
- Requires validation on larger, more diverse patient populations with different hemorrhage types

## Confidence

**High confidence**: Hybrid Retina-UNet architecture and overall methodology are technically sound and well-documented.

**Medium confidence**: Performance metrics (74% recall) are reliable within studied datasets but need validation on larger populations.

**Medium confidence**: Clinical relevance demonstrated but requires prospective clinical studies for full validation.

## Next Checks

1. Validate approach on a significantly larger, multi-center dataset (minimum 100+ cases) with diverse patient demographics and hemorrhage types to assess generalizability.

2. Conduct a prospective clinical study comparing diagnostic accuracy and treatment planning decisions made with and without the voxel scene graph tool, measuring time to diagnosis and clinical outcomes.

3. Expand method to include additional critical brain structures (e.g., basal ganglia, thalamus, brainstem) and test more complex relationship types beyond binary connections.
