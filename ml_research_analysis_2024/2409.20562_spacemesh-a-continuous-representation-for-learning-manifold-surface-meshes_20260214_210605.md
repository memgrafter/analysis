# SpaceMesh: A Continuous Representation for Learning Manifold Surface Meshes

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2409.20562
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.20562
- Authors: Tianchang Shen, Zhaoshuo Li, Marc Law, Matan Atzmon, Sanja Fidler, James Lucas, Jun Gao, Nicholas Sharp
- Reference count: 25
- Primary result: Introduces a continuous representation for generating manifold polygonal meshes with guaranteed connectivity

## Executive Summary
SpaceMesh presents a novel continuous representation for learning to directly generate manifold polygonal meshes. The key innovation is a per-vertex embedding that parameterizes both edge connectivity (using spacetime distance) and local face connectivity (using cyclic neighbor relationships in a halfedge mesh structure). This approach guarantees manifold output while supporting arbitrary polygonal connectivity. The method is validated through single mesh fitting, collection fitting, and large-scale learning on ABC and ShapeNet datasets, achieving significantly faster convergence than alternatives like DMesh and DSE.

## Method Summary
SpaceMesh uses a continuous representation where each vertex has an embedding that encodes both edge and face connectivity information. Edge connectivity is determined through spacetime distance calculations, while face connectivity uses cyclic neighbor relationships in a halfedge mesh structure. The method can learn to generate both vertex positions and connectivity conditioned on point cloud geometry, enabling mesh repair applications. The approach demonstrates scalability to 2,000 vertices with inference under 2 seconds, and produces meshes with distributions of edge lengths and corner angles closely matching ground truth. While it guarantees manifold connectivity, geometric self-intersections may still occur.

## Key Results
- Achieves Chamfer Distance of 1.39 (10^-3) and F1 score of 0.66 on ABC dataset, compared to 11.31 (10^-3) and 0.47 for OccNet
- Demonstrates significantly faster convergence than DMesh and DSE on benchmark datasets
- Produces meshes with edge length and corner angle distributions closely matching ground truth
- Scales to 2,000 vertices with inference under 2 seconds, faster than autoregressive alternatives

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The spacetime distance representation for edge connectivity creates a continuous embedding space where vertices that should be connected in the mesh are mapped to nearby positions. This allows the model to learn connectivity patterns through standard neural network operations on continuous embeddings, rather than discrete combinatorial optimization. The halfedge mesh structure for face connectivity ensures consistent orientation and manifold properties by enforcing cyclic neighbor relationships. Together, these mechanisms allow the model to generate valid manifold meshes through differentiable operations on continuous representations.

## Architecture Onboarding
**Component Map**: Point Cloud -> Encoder -> Embedding Space -> Connectivity Decoder -> Vertex Position Decoder -> Manifold Mesh

**Critical Path**: The encoder maps input point clouds to continuous vertex embeddings, which are then processed by the connectivity decoder to determine edge and face relationships, while the vertex position decoder predicts 3D coordinates. The critical path is: Point Cloud -> Encoder -> Embedding Space -> (Connectivity Decoder + Vertex Position Decoder) -> Mesh Output.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The continuous representation enables differentiable learning of connectivity but may produce geometric self-intersections. The spacetime distance approach provides smooth gradients for learning but requires careful embedding space design. The halfedge structure guarantees manifold properties but adds complexity to the representation.

**Failure Signatures**: Non-manifold outputs indicate embedding space misalignment, geometric self-intersections suggest insufficient regularization on vertex positions, and poor reconstruction quality points to encoder/decoder capacity issues.

**First 3 Experiments**:
1. Verify manifold property preservation by checking Euler characteristic and orientability on generated meshes
2. Compare spacetime distance distributions between connected and unconnected vertex pairs in embedding space
3. Evaluate reconstruction quality on simple geometric primitives (spheres, cubes) before scaling to complex shapes

## Limitations
- The method guarantees manifold connectivity but does not prevent geometric self-intersections, which remains a significant limitation for practical applications
- The claim of faster convergence compared to DMesh and DSE, while demonstrated on specific datasets, may not generalize across all mesh generation tasks
- The representation's scalability to highly complex meshes beyond 2,000 vertices remains untested

## Confidence
**High confidence in**: The mathematical formulation of the spacetime distance representation for edge connectivity and the proof that it guarantees manifold output.

**Medium confidence in**: The quantitative performance improvements on ABC and ShapeNet datasets, as these depend on specific evaluation metrics and dataset characteristics.

**Low confidence in**: The generality of the faster convergence claim across different mesh generation scenarios, as this was demonstrated only on specific benchmark datasets.

## Next Checks
1. Test the method's performance on highly complex meshes with 10,000+ vertices to validate scalability claims beyond the 2,000 vertex limit.
