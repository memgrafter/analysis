# Diffusion Bridge Implicit Models

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2405.15885
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.15885
- Authors: Kaiwen Zheng, Guande He, Jianfei Chen, Fan Bao, Jun Zhu
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: DBIMs achieve up to 25x speedup over DDBMs with deterministic sampling, achieving FID 3.91 on ImageNet 256x256 inpainting with 100 steps

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces diffusion bridge implicit models (DBIMs) to accelerate sampling in denoising diffusion bridge models (DDBMs) without additional training. The key innovation is generalizing DDBMs by introducing a non-Markovian diffusion bridge with a variance parameter, enabling a spectrum of generative processes from stochastic to deterministic. The deterministic variant is particularly efficient, achieving up to 25x speedup over DDBMs. DBIMs also induce a novel form of ordinary differential equation (ODE) that connects to DDBMs' probability flow ODE while being simpler and more efficient, inspiring high-order numerical solvers.

## Method Summary
DBIMs generalize DDBMs by introducing a variance parameter in the diffusion bridge process, allowing control over the stochastic-to-deterministic spectrum. The deterministic variant connects to a novel ODE form that is simpler and more efficient than DDBMs' probability flow ODE. This ODE enables high-order numerical solvers for improved sampling efficiency. The method is demonstrated on image translation and inpainting tasks, achieving state-of-the-art performance with significantly fewer sampling steps. For example, on ImageNet 256x256 inpainting with 128x128 center mask, DBIM with 100 steps achieves FID 3.91, outperforming DDBMs with 500 steps.

## Key Results
- DBIMs achieve up to 25x speedup over DDBMs with deterministic sampling
- On ImageNet 256x256 inpainting with 128x128 center mask, DBIM with 100 steps achieves FID 3.91, outperforming DDBMs with 500 steps
- Deterministic sampling enables faithful encoding, reconstruction, and semantic interpolation

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
DBIMs work by introducing a variance parameter that controls the stochastic-to-deterministic spectrum of the diffusion bridge process. This allows for a more efficient sampling process, particularly in the deterministic regime. The novel ODE form induced by DBIMs is simpler and more efficient than DDBMs' probability flow ODE, enabling high-order numerical solvers that further improve sampling efficiency.

Component map: DDBM -> DBIM (with variance parameter) -> Novel ODE form -> High-order numerical solvers -> Efficient sampling
Critical path: The variance parameter in DBIMs controls the stochastic-to-deterministic spectrum, with the deterministic variant being most efficient. The novel ODE form enables high-order numerical solvers for further efficiency gains.
Design tradeoffs: DBIMs sacrifice some of the flexibility of DDBMs for improved sampling efficiency, particularly in the deterministic regime.
