# Diffusion based multi-domain neuroimaging harmonization method with preservation of anatomical details

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2409.00807
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.00807
- Reference count: 24
- Key outcome: Diffusion-based multi-domain harmonization with single-model efficiency and anatomical preservation

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces a diffusion-based approach for harmonizing neuroimaging data across multiple domains while preserving anatomical details. The method leverages denoising diffusion probabilistic models with a learned domain-invariant condition to maintain structural integrity during harmonization. By implementing skip sampling, the approach achieves computational efficiency and enables harmonization across multiple domains using a single model, addressing key limitations of previous GAN-based methods that required separate models for each domain pair.

## Method Summary
The approach uses denoising diffusion probabilistic models (DDPMs) to harmonize multi-domain neuroimaging data. It employs skip sampling to reduce the number of denoising steps, improving computational efficiency. The method learns domain-invariant conditions to preserve anatomical details while harmonizing textural differences across domains. Unlike previous methods that require separate models for each domain pair, this single-model approach can handle multiple domains simultaneously by learning different diffusion trajectories controlled by domain embeddings.

## Key Results
- Achieved superior FID scores compared to CycleGAN and Seg-Renorm baselines on ADNI1 and ABIDE II datasets
- Demonstrated improved consistency in perivascular space segmentation across different scanners
- Successfully harmonized images across multiple domains using a single diffusion model with skip sampling

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method works by learning a domain-invariant condition that guides the diffusion process to preserve anatomical structures while harmonizing textural variations. The skip sampling technique reduces computational steps without sacrificing quality, enabling efficient multi-domain harmonization. The diffusion model learns multiple trajectories corresponding to different domain transformations, allowing flexible adaptation to various source-target domain pairs.

## Architecture Onboarding

Component Map:
Image -> Diffusion Model -> Skip Sampling -> Domain Embedding -> Harmonized Image

Critical Path:
Input image undergoes diffusion process guided by domain embedding, with skip sampling reducing intermediate steps while maintaining quality.

Design Tradeoffs:
- Computational efficiency vs. harmonization quality in skip sampling
- Model complexity vs. ability to handle multiple domains
- Preservation of fine anatomical details vs. effective texture harmonization

Failure Signatures:
- Loss of anatomical details in extreme domain shifts
- Artifacts introduced by aggressive skip sampling
- Inconsistent harmonization across different domain pairs

First Experiments:
1. Test on small dataset with known domain variations
2. Evaluate skip sampling rates on computational efficiency
3. Validate anatomical preservation on simple structures
