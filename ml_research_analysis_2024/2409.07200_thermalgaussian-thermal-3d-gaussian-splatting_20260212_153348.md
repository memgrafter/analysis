# ThermalGaussian: Thermal 3D Gaussian Splatting

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2409.07200
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.07200
- **Reference count**: 17
- **Primary result**: First 3D Gaussian Splatting approach for multimodal RGB-thermal rendering with 90% storage reduction and 1+ dB PSNR improvement

## Executive Summary
ThermalGaussian introduces the first 3D Gaussian Splatting approach for rendering both RGB and thermal images from novel viewpoints. The method addresses multimodal thermal scene reconstruction by registering RGB and thermal cameras, initializing multimodal 3D Gaussians, and applying multimodal regularization constraints to prevent overfitting and reduce model storage by 90%. The approach introduces a thermal-specific loss function and three types of thermal Gaussian representations, demonstrating significant improvements over baseline methods on a newly contributed RGBT-Scenes dataset.

## Method Summary
The method first calibrates and registers RGB and thermal cameras to establish their geometric relationship. Multimodal SfM strategies are then applied to obtain accurate camera poses for both modalities. Three thermal Gaussian strategies are explored: MSMG (multiple separate modalities Gaussian), OMMG (one multimodal Gaussian), and MFTG (multimodal fused thermal Gaussian). The system uses joint optimization with multimodal regularization that dynamically balances RGB and thermal training, preventing overfitting to either modality. A thermal-specific loss function includes L1 loss, LD-SSIM, and a smoothness term that accounts for thermal images' low texture and smoother temperature distributions.

## Key Results
- PSNR improvements of over 1 dB for both RGB and thermal modalities compared to baselines
- Model storage reduced to 8% of separate modality training through multimodal regularization
- Three thermal Gaussian strategies provide different tradeoffs between training speed, storage efficiency, and rendering quality
- The RGBT-Scenes dataset contains 10 different scenes with over 1000 multimodal images total

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: Multimodal regularization dynamically balances RGB and thermal Gaussian training to prevent overfitting to either modality
- Mechanism: The regularization coefficient γ is computed based on the ratio of Gaussians generated for each modality, adjusting the loss weights so that if one modality dominates, the other modality's loss weight increases
- Core assumption: The number of Gaussians generated for each modality correlates with the modality's training weight, and dynamically adjusting this weight prevents overfitting
- Evidence anchors:
  - [section] "We observe that the weight coefficients of a modality align linearly with the Gaussian it ultimately generates"
  - [abstract] "With the proposed multimodal regularization constraints, we also reduced the model's storage cost by 90%"

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: Thermal-specific loss functions improve reconstruction quality for thermal images by accounting for their unique characteristics
- Mechanism: The thermal loss includes a smoothness term that regularizes against abrupt changes in temperature values, reflecting the physical reality that objects at thermal equilibrium have smoother temperature distributions
- Core assumption: Thermal images have inherently smoother characteristics than RGB images due to thermal equilibrium properties
- Evidence anchors:
  - [section] "We introduce a smoothness term for regularization: Lsmooth = 1/4M Σi,j(|Ti±1,j − Ti,j| + |Ti,j±1 − Ti,j|)"
  - [abstract] "We also develop smoothing constraints tailored to the physical characteristics of the thermal modality"

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: Multimodal initialization enables thermal reconstruction by overcoming the low-texture problem of thermal images in structure-from-motion
- Mechanism: The system first registers RGB and thermal cameras, then uses multimodal SfM strategies to obtain accurate camera poses for both modalities
- Core assumption: RGB images provide sufficient texture for reliable SfM, and their poses can be transferred to thermal cameras after proper calibration and registration
- Evidence anchors:
  - [section] "We first register the RGB and thermal images and then fuse them... to localize the thermal image camera"
  - [section] "We first calibrate the RGB camera and the thermal camera to ensure that both modalities are accurately aligned"

## Architecture Onboarding

- **Component map**: Camera calibration → Camera registration → Multimodal SfM initialization → Gaussian initialization → Joint optimization with multimodal losses → Rendering and evaluation

- **Critical path**: 1. Camera calibration → 2. Multimodal SfM initialization → 3. Gaussian initialization → 4. Joint optimization with multimodal losses → 5. Rendering and evaluation

- **Design tradeoffs**:
  - Storage vs. quality: Multimodal regularization reduces storage by 90% but may slightly impact reconstruction quality
  - Training complexity: Three different thermal Gaussian strategies (MFTG, MSMG, OMMG) offer different tradeoffs between training speed, storage efficiency, and rendering quality
  - Initialization strategy: Choice of multimodal SfM approach affects robustness across different scene types

- **Failure signatures**:
  - Thermal reconstruction failures: Indicate problems with camera registration, SfM initialization, or thermal loss formulation
  - RGB quality degradation: May signal imbalance in multimodal regularization or insufficient RGB-specific constraints
  - Slow convergence: Could indicate poor initialization or inadequate learning rates for the multimodal setup

- **First 3 experiments**:
  1. Test camera calibration accuracy on a simple checkerboard pattern with known thermal properties
  2. Verify multimodal SfM works on a scene with both rich RGB texture and thermal variation using different initialization strategies
  3. Compare the three thermal Gaussian strategies (MFTG, MSMG, OMMG) on a single scene to understand their tradeoffs

## Limitations
- The effectiveness of multimodal regularization in practice is not fully validated through extensive ablation studies
- The three thermal Gaussian strategies (MFTG, MSMG, OMMG) are described but their comparative advantages and specific implementation details are not fully specified
- The thermal smoothness constraint may oversmooth important thermal boundaries in scenes with genuine sharp temperature transitions

## Confidence

- **High confidence**: The core contribution of extending 3D Gaussian Splatting to multimodal RGB-thermal reconstruction, the necessity of camera registration for multimodal systems, and the general framework of using smoothness constraints for thermal images
- **Medium confidence**: The specific formulation of the thermal loss function and its effectiveness compared to simpler alternatives, the storage reduction claims (90% reduction to 8% of separate training), and the relative performance of the three thermal Gaussian strategies
- **Low confidence**: The dynamic balancing mechanism of multimodal regularization in practice, particularly how well the Gaussian count ratio actually reflects training needs across modalities, and the generalizability of the approach to different thermal camera resolutions and scene types

## Next Checks

1. **Ablation study on multimodal regularization**: Train the same scenes with and without the multimodal regularization, varying the initialization strategies, to quantify the actual impact on both quality and storage efficiency, measuring both PSNR/SSIM improvements and storage reduction.

2. **Thermal smoothness parameter sensitivity**: Systematically vary the λsmooth parameter from 0.1 to 1.0 on a diverse set of thermal scenes to identify the optimal value and test whether the proposed 0.6 value is universally applicable or scene-dependent.

3. **Cross-dataset generalization test**: Apply the trained models from the RGBT-Scenes dataset to an independent thermal-RGB dataset without fine-tuning to assess the method's robustness to different thermal camera characteristics and scene distributions.
