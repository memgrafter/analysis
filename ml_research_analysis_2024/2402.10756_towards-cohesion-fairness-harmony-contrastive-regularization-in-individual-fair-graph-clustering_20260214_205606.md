# Towards Cohesion-Fairness Harmony: Contrastive Regularization in Individual Fair Graph Clustering

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2402.10756
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.10756
- Reference count: 31

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of achieving fair graph clustering that balances cluster cohesion and demographic fairness, an area where existing methods fall short. The authors propose iFairNMTF, a novel individually fair graph clustering model based on symmetric Nonnegative Matrix Tri-Factorization (NMTF) with contrastive fairness regularization. The method introduces a contrastive regularization term that promotes the distribution of similar individuals across clusters while ensuring diverse representation within each cluster, controlled by an adjustable parameter λ that allows customizable trade-offs between clustering performance and fairness.

## Method Summary
The iFairNMTF model employs symmetric Nonnegative Matrix Tri-Factorization as its core clustering framework, enhanced with a contrastive fairness regularization term. This regularization encourages similar individuals (based on sensitive attributes) to be distributed across different clusters while maintaining diverse representation within each cluster. The model includes an adjustable parameter λ that controls the trade-off between clustering performance and fairness objectives. The non-negativity constraints on the factor matrices provide interpretability while the contrastive regularization explicitly addresses individual fairness in graph clustering contexts.

## Key Results
- iFairNMTF demonstrates superior flexibility in achieving both fairness (measured by balance) and clustering quality (measured by modularity/accuracy) compared to state-of-the-art methods
- The method was evaluated on six real-world and three synthetic graph datasets, showing consistent performance across different scenarios
- The adjustable λ parameter enables customizable trade-offs between clustering performance and fairness objectives

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The contrastive regularization mechanism works by explicitly modeling the relationship between similar individuals and their cluster assignments, creating a tension between cohesion (keeping similar nodes together) and fairness (distributing similar individuals across clusters). This dual objective is achieved through the NMTF framework which naturally handles graph structures while the regularization term ensures demographic balance. The non-negativity constraints provide interpretability by ensuring that factor loadings have clear semantic meaning, while the contrastive term specifically addresses individual fairness by considering pairwise relationships between similar nodes.

## Architecture Onboarding

Component map: Graph adjacency matrix -> NMTF decomposition -> Factor matrices (H, S, U) -> Contrastive regularization -> Final clustering assignments

Critical path: Data preprocessing -> NMTF initialization -> Iterative optimization with contrastive regularization -> Cluster assignment extraction

Design tradeoffs: The non-negativity constraint provides interpretability but may limit expressiveness; the adjustable λ parameter offers flexibility but requires careful tuning; contrastive regularization adds computational complexity but improves fairness outcomes

Failure signatures: Poor clustering quality when λ is too high; insufficient fairness when λ is too low; convergence issues with highly imbalanced sensitive attribute distributions

## Limitations

- Method assumes availability of sensitive attributes, which may not be practical or ethical in real-world applications
