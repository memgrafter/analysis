# Regional Expected Improvement for Efficient Trust Region Selection in High-Dimensional Bayesian Optimization

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2412.11456

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of Bayesian optimization (BO) in high-dimensional spaces, where traditional methods suffer from performance degradation due to limited function evaluations. The authors propose Regional Expected Improvement (REI), a novel acquisition function designed to enhance trust-region-based BO by identifying regions likely to contain the global optimum. REI is based on averaging the expected improvement within trust regions, allowing for efficient exploration of optimal trust regions without relying on specific problem characteristics. Theoretical analysis proves that REI effectively identifies optimal trust regions. Empirical evaluations on medium to high-dimensional real-world problems demonstrate that incorporating REI into trust-region-based BO outperforms conventional BO and other high-dimensional BO methods.

## Method Summary
The Regional Expected Improvement (REI) method introduces a novel acquisition function that enhances trust-region-based Bayesian optimization in high-dimensional spaces. The approach works by averaging the expected improvement within trust regions, which allows for efficient exploration of optimal trust regions without relying on specific problem characteristics. REI can be applied to any trust-region-based BO method by replacing the conventional acquisition function. The theoretical analysis proves that REI effectively identifies optimal trust regions, and empirical evaluations demonstrate superior performance compared to conventional BO and other high-dimensional BO methods, particularly when trust regions are restarted multiple times.

## Key Results
- REI effectively identifies optimal trust regions through averaging expected improvement within trust regions
- Incorporating REI into trust-region-based BO outperforms conventional BO and other high-dimensional BO methods
- REI shows particular effectiveness when trust regions are restarted multiple times across different problem settings

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
REI works by focusing the optimization process on trust regions that are most likely to contain the global optimum. Traditional BO methods struggle in high-dimensional spaces because they must explore vast regions with limited function evaluations. REI addresses this by computing the average expected improvement within each trust region, which provides a principled way to select which regions to explore next. This averaging approach allows the method to identify promising regions without requiring problem-specific knowledge or assumptions about the function structure.
