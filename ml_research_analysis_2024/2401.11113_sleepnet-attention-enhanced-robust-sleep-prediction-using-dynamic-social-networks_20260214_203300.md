# SleepNet: Attention-Enhanced Robust Sleep Prediction using Dynamic Social Networks

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID:** 2401.11113
- **Source URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.11113
- **Reference count:** 40
- **Primary result:** Proposed GAN-LSTM model achieves accuracy of 0.83, outperforming benchmark models

## Executive Summary
This study introduces SPAND, a novel system that integrates social network contagion with physiological, phone, and weather data to predict next-day sleep duration. The framework leverages Graph Convolutional Networks (GCN) and Graph Attention Networks (GAN) to capture both local and global social influences on sleep patterns. The proposed GAN-LSTM model demonstrates superior performance compared to traditional approaches, achieving an accuracy of 0.83 in sleep duration prediction.

The research highlights the potential of incorporating social network data into mobile health systems for improved sleep prediction. Through comprehensive robustness analysis, the study shows that attention-based models exhibit greater stability against data perturbations compared to GCN or LSTM-only models. This work represents a significant advancement in understanding how social dynamics can complement physiological and environmental sensing data for health monitoring applications.

## Method Summary
The SPAND system integrates social network data with physiological, phone, and weather data to predict next-day sleep duration labels. The framework employs Graph Convolutional Networks (GCN) and Graph Attention Networks (GAN) to capture social contagion effects. The proposed GAN-LSTM model combines attention mechanisms with LSTM architecture for temporal modeling of sleep patterns. The system processes multimodal data streams to generate sleep duration predictions, with particular emphasis on modeling how social connections influence individual sleep behaviors through dynamic network structures.

## Key Results
- GAN-LSTM model achieves accuracy of 0.83, outperforming benchmark models
- Attention-based models demonstrate higher robustness against data perturbations compared to GCN or LSTM-only models
- Social network features complement physiological and environmental signals for improved sleep prediction performance

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The integration of social network contagion with physiological and environmental data enables the model to capture complex interdependencies between individual sleep patterns and social influences. Graph Convolutional Networks effectively aggregate information from neighboring nodes in the social network, allowing the model to learn how sleep behaviors propagate through social connections. The attention mechanism further enhances this by assigning different weights to social influences based on their relevance, creating a more nuanced representation of social impact on sleep.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map:** Social Network Data -> GCN/GAN Layers -> LSTM Layer -> Sleep Duration Prediction

**Critical Path:** Raw multimodal data → Graph construction → GCN/GAN feature extraction → LSTM temporal modeling → Prediction layer

**Design Tradeoffs:** The architecture balances between social influence modeling (GCN/GAN) and temporal pattern learning (LSTM). Attention mechanisms add computational overhead but improve robustness and prediction accuracy. The multimodal integration requires careful feature engineering to ensure all data types contribute meaningfully.

**Failure Signatures:** Poor social network construction leads to ineffective contagion modeling. Insufficient temporal context in LSTM causes loss of sleep pattern memory. Imbalanced feature scaling across modalities can bias predictions toward dominant data sources. Inadequate attention mechanism design results in poor weighting of social influences.

## Limitations
- Robustness analysis conducted on single dataset without cross-population validation
- Model performance reported only as accuracy without precision, recall, or F1-score metrics
- Lack of detailed methodology for social network construction and influence quantification
