# Quantum Circuit $C^*$-algebra Net

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2404.06218
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.06218
- Reference count: 31
- Primary result: Interaction among quantum circuits improves image classification performance by enabling information sharing

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces quantum circuit C*-algebra nets, a framework that generalizes neural network parameters to C*-algebra values, enabling representation of quantum gates as weight parameters. By introducing interaction among multiple quantum circuits through non-diagonal elements in weight matrices, the model allows circuits to share information and improve generalization performance. The approach is applied to encode classical data into quantum states, enabling integration of classical data into quantum algorithms. Numerical experiments on image classification tasks demonstrate that interaction among circuits significantly improves performance, with the encoded data also proving useful for downstream quantum machine learning tasks.

## Method Summary
The quantum circuit C*-algebra net encodes classical images into quantum states using MLPs, then processes these states through a 4-layer quantum circuit C*-algebra net with unitary matrices as parameters. The model uses Adam optimizer on the Stiefel manifold for unitary parameters, with normalizing activation functions to maintain quantum state validity. For image classification, the network is trained with mean squared loss between output and labels. For quantum kernel experiments, the encoded states are used to construct quantum kernels based on fidelity, which are then classified using SVM. The key innovation is the introduction of interaction among multiple circuits through non-diagonal elements in the weight matrices, allowing information sharing between circuits.

## Key Results
- Interaction among quantum circuits improves image classification accuracy compared to independent circuits
- The encoded quantum states from the C*-algebra net are useful for downstream quantum machine learning tasks using SVM with quantum kernels
- The framework successfully generalizes classical data into quantum states that can be used in quantum algorithms

  1. Encode classical data into initial quantum states using MLP
