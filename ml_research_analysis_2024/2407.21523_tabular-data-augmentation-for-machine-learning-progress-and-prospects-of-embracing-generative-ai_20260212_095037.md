# Tabular Data Augmentation for Machine Learning: Progress and Prospects of Embracing Generative AI

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2407.21523

## Executive Summary
This paper provides a comprehensive survey of tabular data augmentation (TDA) methods for machine learning, with particular emphasis on the emerging role of generative AI. The authors introduce a systematic three-stage pipeline for TDA—pre-augmentation (data preparation and indexing), augmentation (retrieval-based and generation-based methods at multiple granularities), and post-augmentation (evaluation and optimization). The survey compares traditional retrieval-based approaches that enrich data by accessing external table pools with modern generation-based methods that synthesize new data using advanced models including GANs, VAEs, diffusion models, and large language models. Key trends identified include enhanced table representation, the convergence of retrieval and generation approaches, and increasing automation of TDA processes. The paper also discusses significant challenges including scalability, interpretability, privacy concerns, and domain-specific applications.

## Method Summary
The survey systematically categorizes tabular data augmentation methods into a three-stage pipeline architecture. Pre-augmentation involves data preparation and indexing to ensure data quality and accessibility. The augmentation stage is divided into retrieval-based methods that query external table repositories and generation-based methods that create synthetic data using generative models. These methods operate at multiple granularities: row-level, column-level, cell-level, and table-level augmentation. Post-augmentation encompasses evaluation metrics and optimization strategies to assess the quality and utility of augmented data. The survey provides comprehensive coverage of both traditional and emerging generative AI approaches, analyzing their strengths, limitations, and applicability across different tabular data scenarios.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The effectiveness of generative AI in tabular data augmentation stems from its ability to learn complex data distributions and generate synthetic samples that preserve statistical properties while expanding dataset diversity. Unlike traditional rule-based or statistical augmentation methods, generative models can capture intricate relationships between features, handle mixed data types, and generate contextually relevant synthetic data. The convergence of retrieval and generation approaches leverages the strengths of both paradigms: retrieval methods provide domain-specific, high-quality examples from curated repositories, while generation methods offer unlimited scalability and customization. This hybrid approach addresses the fundamental challenge of data scarcity in tabular domains while maintaining data integrity and relevance for downstream machine learning tasks.

## Architecture Onboarding
- **Component map**: Data Collection -> Pre-augmentation (Preparation + Indexing) -> Augmentation (Retrieval-based + Generation-based at row/column/cell/table levels) -> Post-augmentation (Evaluation + Optimization) -> Model Training
- **Critical path**: Data preparation → Table representation learning → Augmentation method selection → Synthetic data generation → Quality evaluation → Model integration
- **Design tradeoffs**: Retrieval methods offer high-quality, domain-specific examples but limited scalability; generation methods provide unlimited synthetic data but may struggle with data integrity and interpretability; hybrid approaches balance these tradeoffs
- **Failure signatures**: Poor data integrity (invalid synthetic samples), loss of statistical properties, privacy leakage in synthetic data, scalability bottlenecks in generation methods, retrieval irrelevance or noise introduction
- **First experiments**: 1) Benchmark traditional augmentation methods against generation-based approaches on small tabular datasets; 2) Evaluate data integrity preservation using GANs on mixed-type tabular data; 3) Test hybrid retrieval-generation pipeline on domain-specific datasets

## Limitations
- Generative AI methods for tabular data remain largely experimental with limited standardized benchmarks
- Many generation-based approaches struggle with maintaining data integrity and preserving statistical properties
- The convergence of retrieval and generation approaches is theoretical with limited empirical validation
- Privacy and scalability challenges are identified but lack quantitative analysis of computational costs
