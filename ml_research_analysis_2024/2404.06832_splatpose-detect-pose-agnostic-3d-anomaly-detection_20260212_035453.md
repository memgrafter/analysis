# SplatPose & Detect: Pose-Agnostic 3D Anomaly Detection

## Quick Facts

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces SplatPose, a 3D Gaussian splatting-based framework for pose-agnostic anomaly detection in industrial settings. The method addresses the challenge of detecting defects in 3D objects captured from varying viewpoints by accurately estimating the pose of unseen views in a differentiable manner. SplatPose leverages 3D Gaussian splatting to encode multi-view images of objects, enabling real-time rendering and pose estimation. The framework aligns query images with defect-free renderings to detect anomalies, achieving state-of-the-art performance in both detection and segmentation tasks. Notably, SplatPose outperforms competing methods, including OmniposeAD, by significant margins in accuracy while being 55 times faster in training and 13 times faster in inference. The method also demonstrates robustness in sparse-view settings, requiring only 60% of the training data to achieve superior results. Overall, SplatPose sets a new benchmark for pose-agnostic anomaly detection in industrial applications.

## Method Summary
SplatPose addresses pose-agnostic 3D anomaly detection by combining 3D Gaussian splatting with differentiable pose estimation. The framework learns to reconstruct defect-free objects from multi-view images and estimate the pose of unseen query images. During inference, it aligns the query image with a defect-free rendering of the same object from the estimated pose, and anomalies are detected based on reconstruction error. The method uses a self-supervised training approach, requiring only defect-free training data, and leverages the efficiency of 3D Gaussian splatting for real-time rendering and pose estimation. SplatPose achieves state-of-the-art performance on industrial anomaly detection benchmarks while significantly reducing computational costs compared to existing methods.

- **Sparse-view learning**: Demonstrates effectiveness with limited training data, reducing data collection requirements

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Multi-view images -> 3D Gaussian splatting encoder -> Pose estimator -> Renderer -> Alignment module -> Anomaly detector

**Critical Path**: The core pipeline processes multi-view images through a 3D Gaussian splatting encoder to learn a 3D representation, estimates the pose of query images, renders defect-free views from the estimated poses, and detects anomalies by comparing query images with aligned renderings.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The method prioritizes speed and accuracy over model complexity, using 3D Gaussian splatting for efficient rendering. This choice enables real-time performance but may limit handling of extremely complex geometries. The pose-agnostic approach trades some potential accuracy for robustness across viewing angles.

**Failure Signatures**: Performance may degrade with highly reflective surfaces, complex geometries, or textures similar to defects. Extreme viewing angles or severe occlusions could also challenge the pose estimation module.
