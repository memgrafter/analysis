# Deciphering Oracle Bone Language with Diffusion Models

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2406.00684
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.00684
- **Reference count**: 12
- **Primary result**: OBSD achieves top-1 accuracy of 41.0% and 30.0% using custom-built OBS-OCR and PaddleOCR tools respectively

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces OBSD, a diffusion-based model for deciphering Oracle Bone Script (OBS), an ancient Chinese writing system that presents unique challenges for traditional NLP methods due to the scarcity of large text corpora. The model employs a conditional diffusion strategy that generates images of modern Chinese characters as decipherment clues, combined with Localized Structural Sampling (LSS) to capture local radical structures and a zero-shot refinement stage to enhance accuracy. Experimental results on an OBS dataset demonstrate that OBSD outperforms state-of-the-art image-to-image translation methods, showing promise for AI-assisted decipherment of ancient languages.

## Method Summary
OBSD uses a diffusion-based approach specifically designed for the challenging task of deciphering Oracle Bone Script. The model generates modern Chinese character images as decipherment clues through a conditional diffusion strategy, addressing the lack of large text corpora that typically limits traditional NLP methods. Localized Structural Sampling (LSS) is incorporated to learn and capture local radical structures inherent in the characters, while a zero-shot refinement stage further improves the accuracy of generated decipherments. The approach leverages the evolutionary relationship between Oracle Bone characters and modern Chinese characters, using diffusion models to bridge the gap between these related writing systems.

## Key Results
- OBSD achieves top-1 accuracy of 41.0% using custom-built OBS-OCR tool
- OBSD achieves top-1 accuracy of 30.0% using PaddleOCR tool
- Outperforms state-of-the-art image-to-image translation methods on OBS dataset

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The model leverages diffusion models' ability to generate high-quality images conditioned on Oracle Bone Script inputs, using modern Chinese characters as the target domain. By incorporating Localized Structural Sampling (LSS), the model can capture and preserve the structural features of radicals that are critical for character recognition and decipherment. The zero-shot refinement stage allows for iterative improvement without requiring additional labeled data, which is particularly valuable given the limited available resources for ancient scripts. The approach assumes a structural continuity between Oracle Bone characters and their modern descendants, enabling the model to learn meaningful mappings even with limited training data.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Oracle Bone Image -> Localized Structural Sampler -> Diffusion Generator -> Modern Character Image -> OCR Validator

**Critical Path**: The sequence Oracle Bone Image → Localized Structural Sampler → Diffusion Generator → Modern Character Image represents the core inference pipeline, with the OCR Validator providing feedback for the zero-shot refinement stage.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The model trades off between generation quality and structural fidelity, prioritizing the preservation of radical structures through LSS while accepting some potential loss in fine-grained stroke accuracy. The reliance on modern Chinese character images as targets assumes evolutionary continuity that may not hold for all characters. The zero-shot refinement approach avoids the need for labeled ancient script data but may be limited by the quality of the initial generation.

**Failure Signatures**: Poor decipherment results may manifest as generated characters that share visual features with the input but lack semantic correspondence, or as outputs that fail to preserve critical radical structures. Performance degradation could occur when Oracle Bone characters have no clear modern descendants or when the structural evolution between scripts is non-linear. The model may also struggle with characters that underwent significant simplification or radical replacement during script evolution.

## Limitations
- Evaluation relies on OCR-based accuracy metrics that may not fully capture semantic correctness of decipherments
- Lack of ground truth semantic labels or expert validation for generated decipherments
- Performance claims lack detailed comparison methodology with baseline image-to-image translation methods
- Model assumes direct evolutionary relationship between Oracle Bone and modern characters that may not always hold
