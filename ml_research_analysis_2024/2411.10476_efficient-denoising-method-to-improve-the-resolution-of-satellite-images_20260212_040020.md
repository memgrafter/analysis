# Efficient Denoising Method to Improve The Resolution of Satellite Images

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2411.10476
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.10476
- Authors: Jhanavi Hegde
- Reference count: 0
- Primary result: CM-based method achieved 4 dB PSNR improvement in 60% of satellite images while reducing denoising time by 20x

## Executive Summary
This work proposes a method to enhance the resolution of satellite images using a computationally efficient Consistency Model (CM) built on Stable Diffusion. The approach converts the stochastic denoising diffusion process in Stable Diffusion into a deterministic one via Teacher-Student Distillation, enabling faster super-resolution with fewer steps. Using the DOTA v2.0 dataset, the model improved the FID score from 10.0 to 1.9 and achieved up to 4 dB PSNR improvement in 60% of images.

The CM-based method demonstrated strong potential for improving satellite image preprocessing in climate monitoring applications while reducing computational costs by a factor of 20 compared to traditional stochastic methods.

## Method Summary
The method leverages Stable Diffusion's denoising diffusion framework but converts it from stochastic to deterministic through Teacher-Student Distillation. This architectural modification allows the model to achieve comparable image quality with significantly fewer denoising steps. The approach was trained on the DOTA v2.0 dataset and evaluated using standard metrics including FID and PSNR scores.

## Key Results
- FID score improved from 10.0 to 1.9
- Achieved up to 4 dB PSNR improvement in 60% of images
- Reduced denoising time by 20x compared to traditional stochastic methods

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method works by converting the inherently stochastic denoising diffusion process into a deterministic one through Teacher-Student Distillation. In traditional diffusion models, each denoising step involves sampling from a probability distribution, which introduces randomness and requires multiple steps to converge to a stable result. By distilling this process into a deterministic model, the CM can achieve similar quality with fewer steps and reduced computational overhead.

## Limitations
- 4 dB PSNR improvement claim based on single dataset (DOTA v2.0) and only 60% of images
- 20x speedup claim needs verification across diverse satellite image resolutions and computational environments
- Lack of ablation studies to quantify impact of stochastic-to-deterministic conversion on final image quality
