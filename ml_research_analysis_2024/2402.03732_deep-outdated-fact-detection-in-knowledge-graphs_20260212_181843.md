# Deep Outdated Fact Detection in Knowledge Graphs

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2402.03732
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.03732
- Reference count: 36
- Key outcome: DEAN achieves 5-10% accuracy improvements over baselines for outdated fact detection in knowledge graphs

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of detecting outdated facts in knowledge graphs, where real-world changes render stored information obsolete. The authors propose DEAN (Deep outdatEd fAct detectioN), a deep learning framework that combines graph attention networks with a contrastive learning approach based on a Relations-to-Nodes graph. The framework demonstrates superior performance compared to state-of-the-art methods, particularly excelling on datasets with diverse relation types.

## Method Summary
DEAN employs a three-module architecture: a Fact Attention Module using graph attention networks to learn entity embeddings, a Contrastive R2N Module that builds and processes a Relations-to-Nodes graph with GCN and contrastive learning, and a Detection Module for binary classification of outdated facts. The framework is trained jointly with a weighted combination of attention and contrastive losses, using TransE embeddings as initialization. Experiments are conducted on six datasets with synthetic outdated facts injected at 20% of the data.

## Key Results
- DEAN achieves 5-10% accuracy improvements over baseline methods across multiple datasets
- Performance particularly excels on datasets with larger numbers of relation types (FB15K-237, Kinship, UMLS)
- The framework shows effectiveness in learning relation features for outdated fact detection

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
The fact attention module captures implicit structural information in KGs by learning fact embeddings through multi-head attention over entity-relation-entity triples. For each entity, the module computes attention scores over all facts connected to it, using learnable linear transformations and a LeakyReLU activation. These scores weight the contributions of each fact embedding to produce an updated entity representation.

### Mechanism 2
The contrastive R2N module improves relation feature learning by leveraging a pre-defined Relations-to-Nodes graph with GCN-based message passing and contrastive learning. The R2N graph encodes co-occurrence patterns among relations based on shared entities. A GCN aggregates neighbor information, and a contrastive objective pushes apart positive and negative relation samples to enhance discriminativeness.

### Mechanism 3
Joint training of the fact attention and contrastive R2N modules yields better outdated fact detection than either module alone. The two modules produce complementary entity and relation embeddings; their weighted sum forms the final loss, which is optimized jointly with the binary classification loss.

## Foundational Learning

- Graph attention networks
  - Why needed here: To aggregate information from a node's neighborhood in a way that emphasizes more relevant structural connections for outdated fact detection
  - Quick check question: How does multi-head attention stabilize the learning process compared to single-head attention?

- Contrastive learning
  - Why needed here: To force the model to learn discriminative relation features by contrasting positive and negative samples in the R2N graph
  - Quick check question: What is the role of the discriminator in the contrastive R2N module's objective?

- Knowledge graph embedding
  - Why needed here: To generate initial entity and relation representations that encode basic structural information before the attention and contrastive modules refine them
  - Quick check question: Why is TransE used as the initial embedding method before applying attention?

## Architecture Onboarding

- Component map: Input KG -> TransE embeddings -> Fact Attention -> Contrastive R2N -> Detection -> Output
- Critical path: The framework processes the knowledge graph through TransE initialization, then applies attention-based fact aggregation followed by R2N-based relation learning before final classification
- Design tradeoffs: Using TransE for initialization is simple but may not capture complex relation patterns; alternatives like ComplEx could be considered. The R2N graph is static; dynamic updates could improve performance but add complexity
- Failure signatures: Degraded accuracy on datasets with few relation types indicates the R2N module may be underutilized. Overfitting on small datasets suggests the attention module may be memorizing rather than generalizing
- First 3 experiments:
  1. Ablation study: remove the contrastive R2N module and measure impact on FB15K-237 accuracy
  2. Hyperparameter sweep: vary the number of attention heads (K) and observe stability across datasets
  3. Dataset complexity test: compare performance on Nations (few entities/relations) vs. FB15K-237 (many relations)

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out
- How does the DEAN framework perform when applied to datasets with a large number of entities but few relation types?
- Can the DEAN framework be extended to detect outdated facts caused by changes in entities, in addition to changes in relations?
- How does the performance of DEAN scale with increasing dataset size, particularly in terms of time complexity?

## Limitations
- Performance advantage is less clear on datasets with fewer relation types (Nations)
- Construction details of the Relations-to-Nodes graph remain underspecified
- Synthetic nature of outdated facts may not fully capture real-world knowledge obsolescence patterns

## Confidence
- Performance on relation-rich datasets: High
- Generalization to diverse KG structures: Medium-High
- Reproducibility of R2N graph construction: Medium
- Real-world applicability: Medium

## Next Checks
1. Evaluate DEAN on a real-world KG with known fact obsolescence (e.g., Wikidata with time-stamped updates) rather than synthetic outdated facts to assess practical applicability
2. Conduct a detailed ablation study varying the construction parameters of the Relations-to-Nodes graph to quantify its impact on performance across different dataset characteristics
3. Test DEAN's performance under different levels of noise injection and varying proportions of outdated facts to establish its reliability threshold in real-world scenarios
