# A Troublemaker with Contagious Jailbreak Makes Chaos in Honest Towns

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2410.16155
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.16155
- Reference count: 25
- Primary result: ARCJ improves jailbreak attack success rates by 23.51%, 18.95%, and 52.93% across different agent topologies

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of attacking multi-agent systems where agents have independent memory, focusing on the "toxicity disappearing" phenomenon where poisoned information loses effectiveness during agent communication. The authors propose the Adversarial Replication Contagious Jailbreak (ARCJ) method, which optimizes retrieval suffixes for easier retrieval and replication suffixes for contagious propagation. ARCJ demonstrates significant improvements in jailbreak success rates across various agent topologies while maintaining practical deployment efficiency.

## Method Summary
ARCJ employs a two-stage optimization framework that addresses the "toxicity disappearing" problem in multi-agent systems. The method first optimizes retrieval suffixes to ensure successful jailbreak message retrieval, then optimizes replication suffixes to enhance contagious propagation across agent networks. The approach uses Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) with a two-step optimization process, achieving a balance between attack effectiveness and computational efficiency. The method is evaluated on the Troublemaker Makes Chaos in Honest Towns (TMCHT) task across graph, line, and star topologies.

## Key Results
- 23.51% improvement in attack success rates for line topology compared to baselines
- 18.95% improvement in attack success rates for star topology
- 52.93% improvement in attack success rates in 100-agent settings

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
ARCJ works by systematically addressing the propagation challenges in multi-agent systems through specialized suffix optimization. The retrieval optimization ensures jailbreak content is easily found, while replication optimization ensures the content spreads effectively through the agent network. The two-stage approach with PPO optimization creates a feedback loop that continuously improves both retrieval and propagation capabilities.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map:**
Base LLM -> Retrieval Suffix Optimizer -> Replication Suffix Optimizer -> Attack Evaluation

**Critical Path:**
Jailbreak prompt generation → Retrieval suffix optimization → Agent communication → Replication suffix optimization → Attack success evaluation

**Design Tradeoffs:**
- Computational efficiency vs. attack success rate
- Prefix optimization complexity vs. practical deployment
- Generalizability vs. task-specific optimization
- Detection avoidance vs. propagation effectiveness

**Failure Signatures:**
- Poor retrieval suffix optimization leads to low initial success rates
- Ineffective replication suffixes cause "toxicity disappearing"
- Over-optimized suffixes may be detectable by defensive systems
- Communication bottlenecks in dense agent networks

## Limitations
- Results are confined to the TMCHT task and specific agent topologies
- Evaluation focuses primarily on success rates without extensive analysis of side effects
- Reliance on LLaMA-3-8B base model raises questions about performance transfer to other LLM architectures
- Limited analysis of detection probability and defensive countermeasures
