# DecKG: Decentralized Collaborative Learning with Knowledge Graph Enhancement for POI Recommendation

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2410.10130
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.10130
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Achieves up to 0.0389 NDCG@10 and 0.0559 Recall@20 on Beijing dataset for privacy-preserving POI recommendation

## Executive Summary
DecKG addresses privacy-preserving POI recommendation by integrating knowledge graph enhancement with decentralized collaborative learning. The method generates desensitized check-in data by uploading general categories rather than specific POI IDs, enabling privacy-preserving neighbor identification. A server pretrains the entire knowledge graph without sensitive user-item interactions and deploys relevant sub-KGs to individual users, who then refine entities on-device and exchange knowledge with neighbors.

## Method Summary
DecKG combines knowledge graph enhancement with decentralized collaborative learning to protect user privacy in POI recommendation. The approach uploads general categories of interacted items instead of specific POI IDs, allowing for privacy-preserving neighbor identification through category-based sampling. The server pretrains the entire knowledge graph without sensitive user-item interactions, then partitions and deploys relevant sub-knowledge graphs to individual users. Users refine entities on-device and exchange knowledge with neighbors in a decentralized manner, enabling collaborative learning while preserving privacy.

## Key Results
- Achieves up to 0.0389 NDCG@10 and 0.0559 Recall@20 on Beijing dataset
- Demonstrates significant performance improvements over federated and decentralized baselines
- Successfully maintains privacy through category-level data sharing rather than revealing specific POI interactions

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
DecKG works by creating a privacy-preserving framework that balances recommendation accuracy with data protection. By generalizing POI interactions to category levels, the method reduces the risk of exposing sensitive user behavior while still enabling meaningful collaborative learning. The knowledge graph partitioning strategy ensures that users receive relevant information tailored to their interests without accessing the entire dataset. The decentralized architecture allows for knowledge exchange between neighbors while maintaining individual privacy through local entity refinement.

## Architecture Onboarding
Component Map: User Devices -> Local Entity Refinement -> Neighbor Communication -> Server (for initial KG pretraining)
Critical Path: Category upload → Neighbor identification → KG partitioning → Local refinement → Knowledge exchange
Design Tradeoffs: Privacy vs. recommendation accuracy, granularity of category levels, balance between local and collaborative learning
Failure Signatures: Poor recommendation quality from over-generalization, neighbor discovery failures from sparse category data, knowledge graph fragmentation issues
First Experiments:

## Limitations
- Category-level generalization may limit recommendation granularity and accuracy
- Knowledge graph partitioning effectiveness not fully explored for heterogeneous user interests
- Scalability to larger knowledge graphs and complex POI categories remains unclear

## Confidence
- Privacy preservation through category-based data sharing: High
- Knowledge graph enhancement improving recommendation accuracy: Medium
- Decentralized collaborative learning outperforming federated baselines: Medium
- Scalability and robustness to sparse or heterogeneous data: Low

## Next Checks
1. Evaluate the impact of category-level generalization on recommendation accuracy across diverse POI datasets with varying levels of category granularity
2. Assess the scalability of DecKG by testing its performance on larger knowledge graphs and datasets with millions of POIs and users
3. Investigate the robustness of DecKG against adversarial attacks, such as data poisoning or manipulated category assignments, to ensure privacy and recommendation integrity
