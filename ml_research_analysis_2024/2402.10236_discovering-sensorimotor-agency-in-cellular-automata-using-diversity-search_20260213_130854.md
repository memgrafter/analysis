# Discovering Sensorimotor Agency in Cellular Automata using Diversity Search

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2402.10236
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.10236
- Reference count: 0
- Key outcome: Approach to automatically discover robust sensorimotor agents in cellular automata using diversity search, curriculum learning, and gradient descent

## Executive Summary
This paper presents a method to automatically discover sensorimotor agents in cellular automata (CA) through diversity search, curriculum learning, and gradient descent. The authors address the challenge of finding environmental rules that self-organize robust "individuals" with sensorimotor agency in CA, starting from initial states with no prior existence of bodies, brains, perception, or action. They leverage the Lenia continuous CA model and propose a method based on intrinsically motivated goal exploration processes (IMGEP) to efficiently search for parameters leading to the emergence of moving agents that can react coherently to external obstacles and maintain their integrity.

## Method Summary
The approach uses intrinsically motivated goal exploration processes (IMGEP) combined with curriculum learning and gradient descent to discover environmental rules that generate sensorimotor agents in continuous cellular automata (Lenia). The method iteratively searches for CA parameters that produce agents capable of coherent movement and obstacle navigation while maintaining structural integrity. The search process employs diversity maximization as an intrinsic motivation to explore the parameter space efficiently, combined with curriculum strategies that gradually increase environmental complexity during training.

## Key Results
- Discovered agents exhibit robust capabilities to move, maintain body integrity, and navigate among various obstacles
- Agents demonstrate strong generalization abilities, showing robustness to changes of scale, random updates, and environmental perturbations not seen during training
- The approach systematically finds environmental conditions in CA leading to self-organization of basic forms of sensorimotor agency

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method works by combining diversity search with curriculum learning to efficiently explore the parameter space of cellular automata. By maximizing diversity in discovered behaviors, the approach avoids getting stuck in local optima and finds novel agent configurations. The curriculum learning component gradually increases environmental complexity, allowing agents to develop robustness through progressive challenges. The gradient descent optimization fine-tunes parameters to achieve specific sensorimotor goals while maintaining diversity.

## Foundational Learning
- **Cellular Automata**: Discrete models consisting of grids of cells that evolve according to local rules - needed to understand the basic computational framework being studied
- **Lenia Model**: Continuous cellular automata with smooth state transitions and a large number of possible states per cell - provides the computational substrate for agent discovery
- **Intrinsically Motivated Goal Exploration**: Learning algorithms that use internal rewards to drive exploration of novel behaviors - enables efficient search in complex parameter spaces
- **Curriculum Learning**: Training approach where task difficulty increases progressively - helps build robust agent behaviors through gradual environmental challenges

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Lenia CA parameters -> Diversity Search -> Curriculum Learning -> Gradient Descent -> Sensorimotor Agent

**Critical Path**: The key pipeline involves initializing Lenia parameters, running diversity search to explore the parameter space, applying curriculum learning to increase environmental complexity, and using gradient descent to optimize for sensorimotor behaviors while maintaining diversity.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The approach balances exploration (diversity search) with exploitation (gradient descent), and gradual learning (curriculum) with efficient parameter optimization. Using continuous CA (Lenia) enables smoother agent behaviors but increases computational complexity compared to discrete CA models.

**Failure Signatures**: The search may get stuck in local optima producing non-robust agents, fail to discover coherent moving agents, or generate agents that cannot maintain integrity under environmental perturbations.

**First Experiments**: 
1. Run baseline diversity search without curriculum learning to assess the impact of gradual complexity increase
2. Test agent robustness across different scales and noise levels not seen during training
3. Compare performance with alternative CA models (e.g., discrete CA) to evaluate the benefits of continuous state representation

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out

### Open Question 1
- Question: What are the formal definitions of agency and sensorimotor capabilities that can be applied to high-dimensional systems like the continuous cellular automata studied in this paper?
- Basis in paper: The authors state "several of the analyses we make in this work are empirical estimations or subjective. Future work shall consider how more formal definition(s) of agency and sensorimotor capabilities could be applied to the high-dimensional systems studied here."
- Why unresolved: Current measures rely on empirical tests and subjective criteria. A formal mathematical definition of agency that captures self-maintenance, individuality, and sensorimotor behavior in complex systems is needed.
- What evidence would resolve it: Development and application of formal information-theoretic measures of individuality and agency that can be applied to continuous cellular automata and demonstrate their effectiveness in identifying and characterizing agents.

### Open Question 2
- Question: Under what environmental conditions can open-ended evolutionary processes emerge directly within the cellular automata environment, leading to agents with increasingly complex behaviors?
- Basis in paper: The authors discuss the possibility of engineering open-ended artificial systems with forms of functional life and agency, mentioning "beyond individual capabilities, we could even wonder under what conditions one could observe the emergence of an open-ended evolutionary process directly in the environment."
- Why unresolved: Current work focuses on discovering specific agents through goal-directed search. Understanding the conditions that foster ongoing evolution and increasing complexity within the CA system is an open challenge.
- What evidence would resolve it: Demonstration of cellular automata environments where new species, behaviors, and complexities continuously emerge over time without external intervention.

### Open Question 3
- Question: How can insights from artificial cellular automata agents be transferred to real biological systems or very large AI systems to control complex system-level behaviors?
- Basis in paper: The authors mention "transferring insights from the considered artificial systems to real biological systems or to very large AI systems is an exciting area of research with a potential broad range of medical and societal applications."
- Why unresolved: While the CA model provides a simplified framework for studying agency and self-organization, applying these insights to the vastly more complex real-world systems remains a significant challenge.
- What evidence would resolve it: Successful application of CA-inspired methods to control or influence behaviors in biological systems (e.g., tissue regeneration) or large AI models (e.g., guiding language model outputs), demonstrating practical utility of the approach.

## Limitations
- The evaluation primarily focuses on specific obstacle configurations and movement scenarios, with relatively narrow environmental diversity compared to the full potential space of CA dynamics
- The methodology's effectiveness for discovering agents with sophisticated sensorimotor capabilities or multi-agent interactions beyond basic movement and obstacle avoidance remains untested
- The choice of Lenia as the CA model may limit the generality of findings to other CA frameworks

## Confidence
- Claims about agent robustness and generalization: Medium
- Claims about the systematic discovery of sensorimotor agency: Medium
- Claims about the effectiveness of the diversity search + curriculum learning approach: Medium
