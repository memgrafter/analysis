# Expected Coordinate Improvement for High-Dimensional Bayesian Optimization

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2404.11917
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.11917
- Authors: Dawei Zhan
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Proposed ECI-BO algorithm outperforms standard BO and achieves competitive results compared to five state-of-the-art high-dimensional BO methods on 100-dimensional problems

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces Expected Coordinate Improvement (ECI) as a novel criterion for high-dimensional Bayesian optimization. The ECI-BO algorithm simplifies the infill selection problem by optimizing one coordinate at a time, selecting the coordinate with the highest ECI value in each iteration. This approach reduces the computational complexity of standard BO while maintaining or improving optimization performance on high-dimensional benchmark problems.

## Method Summary
The ECI-BO algorithm addresses high-dimensional optimization by decomposing the problem into a series of one-dimensional optimizations. At each iteration, the algorithm evaluates the expected improvement for each coordinate individually, then selects the coordinate with the maximum expected improvement for optimization. This strategy transforms the complex high-dimensional search into a sequence of simpler one-dimensional problems, significantly reducing computational overhead while maintaining exploration-exploitation balance through the ECI criterion.

## Key Results
- ECI-BO achieves significantly better performance than standard BO on 100-dimensional benchmark problems
- The method demonstrates competitive results compared to five state-of-the-art high-dimensional BO algorithms
- ECI-BO shows faster convergence rates and better final solutions while reducing overall computational time

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The ECI-BO algorithm works by exploiting the structure of high-dimensional spaces through coordinate-wise optimization. By focusing on one dimension at a time, the algorithm reduces the curse of dimensionality inherent in standard BO, where the search space grows exponentially with dimension. The ECI criterion provides a principled way to balance exploration and exploitation at the coordinate level, allowing the algorithm to efficiently navigate the high-dimensional landscape while maintaining the theoretical guarantees of Bayesian optimization.
