# Improving Factuality of 3D Brain MRI Report Generation with Paired Image-domain Retrieval and Text-domain Augmentation

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2411.15490
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.15490
- Reference count: 18
- Outperforms GPT-4o and LLaVA-Med in detecting ischemic territories with up to 94.04% mAP@1 accuracy

## Executive Summary
This study presents PIRTA, a retrieval-augmented generation framework for generating accurate radiology reports from 3D brain MRI data for acute ischemic stroke diagnosis. The framework addresses the challenge of direct image-to-text generation by retrieving similar DWI images with paired ground-truth reports and using them to augment the report generation process. PIRTA employs a pretrained 3D vision transformer for image retrieval and instruction-tuned LLMs for report generation. The method significantly outperforms state-of-the-art multimodal language models in detecting ischemic territories and demonstrates robust generalization across different institutions and scanner vendors.

## Method Summary
PIRTA bypasses the complexity of direct image-to-text generation by employing a retrieval-augmented approach. The framework first retrieves similar DWI images with paired ground-truth reports using a pretrained 3D vision transformer. These retrieved reports are then used to augment the report generation process through instruction-tuned LLMs. The method is specifically designed for acute ischemic stroke diagnosis, focusing on detecting ischemic territories in 3D brain MRI data. The retrieval component leverages similarity in image space to find clinically relevant reference cases, while the text generation component synthesizes these references into coherent, factual reports.

## Key Results
- Achieves 94.04% mAP@1 accuracy on internal datasets for ischemic territory detection
- Significantly outperforms GPT-4o and LLaVA-Med in generating factual radiology reports
- Demonstrates robust generalization across different institutions and scanner vendors
- Successfully detects ischemic territories with higher accuracy than state-of-the-art multimodal language models

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework's effectiveness stems from its retrieval-augmented approach that leverages existing high-quality paired image-report data rather than attempting direct image-to-text generation. By retrieving similar cases with known ground-truth reports, the system can provide more factually accurate and clinically relevant information. The use of pretrained 3D vision transformers enables effective similarity matching in the image domain, while instruction-tuned LLMs can effectively synthesize the retrieved information into coherent reports. This approach reduces the complexity of generating accurate clinical content from scratch by providing relevant reference material.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: 3D MRI Data -> 3D Vision Transformer -> Image Retrieval -> Ground-Truth Report Collection -> Instruction-Tuned LLM -> Generated Report

**Critical Path**: The most time-sensitive components are the 3D vision transformer retrieval and the instruction-tuned LLM generation. Retrieval must find clinically relevant matches quickly to enable real-time report generation, while the LLM synthesis needs to produce coherent reports within acceptable clinical workflow timeframes.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The framework trades direct end-to-end learning for retrieval-augmented generation, sacrificing some speed for improved factuality. This approach requires maintaining a comprehensive database of paired image-report data but significantly reduces hallucination risk compared to direct generation models.

**Failure Signatures**: Performance degradation may occur when retrieved cases are not sufficiently similar to the query image, when the reference database lacks diversity in stroke presentations, or when the LLM fails to properly synthesize retrieved information. Limited database coverage of rare stroke patterns could also impact accuracy.

## Limitations
- Relies entirely on internal datasets for validation without independent external testing
- Performance depends on the quality and representativeness of the paired image-report database
- Evaluation focuses primarily on ischemic territory detection rather than full report clinical completeness
- Computational requirements for 3D vision transformer and LLM may limit practical deployment
