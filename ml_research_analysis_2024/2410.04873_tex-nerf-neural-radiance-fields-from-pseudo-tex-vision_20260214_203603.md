# TeX-NeRF: Neural Radiance Fields from Pseudo-TeX Vision
## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID:** 2410.04873
- **Source URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.04873
- **Reference count:** 34
- **Primary result:** Novel 3D reconstruction method using only infrared images with superior visual quality and temperature accuracy

## Executive Summary
TeX-NeRF introduces a novel approach to 3D reconstruction that leverages infrared imagery, overcoming the limitations of visible-light cameras in low-light or adverse weather conditions. The method innovatively uses object material emissivity as a priori knowledge and preprocesses infrared images through Pseudo-TeX vision to extract temperature, emissivity, and texture information. These extracted features are mapped into HSV color channels to guide the neural radiance field reconstruction process. The approach demonstrates significant improvements in both visual quality metrics and temperature estimation accuracy compared to existing methods.

## Method Summary
TeX-NeRF processes infrared images by first extracting temperature (T), emissivity (e), and texture (X) information through a Pseudo-TeX vision preprocessing step. These three components are then mapped to the HSV color space channels, where hue represents temperature, saturation represents emissivity, and value represents texture. This HSV representation is used to condition a neural radiance field, enabling 3D reconstruction from infrared data alone. The method explicitly incorporates material emissivity as a priori knowledge, which is critical for accurate temperature estimation and reconstruction quality in the infrared domain.

## Key Results
- Outperforms existing methods in visual quality metrics (PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS) for novel view synthesis
- Achieves superior temperature estimation accuracy with lower MAE compared to baseline approaches
- Produces more detailed and complete 3D point clouds than traditional reconstruction techniques

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
TeX-NeRF works by leveraging the physical properties of infrared radiation and material emissivity. Objects emit infrared radiation based on their temperature and emissivity, which are material-dependent properties. By extracting these physical properties from infrared images and using them as explicit inputs to the neural radiance field, the method can better reconstruct the 3D geometry and appearance of objects. The HSV mapping strategy effectively encodes the physical properties in a way that neural networks can process, while the Pseudo-TeX vision preprocessing step provides the necessary feature extraction from raw infrared data.

## Architecture Onboarding
**Component Map:** Infrared Images -> Pseudo-TeX Vision -> T, e, X Extraction -> HSV Mapping -> NeRF Network -> 3D Reconstruction

**Critical Path:** The preprocessing pipeline (Pseudo-TeX vision) directly impacts reconstruction quality, making it the most critical component. Errors in extracting temperature, emissivity, or texture will propagate through the entire system and degrade final results.

**Design Tradeoffs:** The method trades computational complexity in preprocessing for improved reconstruction quality in challenging conditions. While visible-light methods may be faster, TeX-NeRF provides superior performance in low-light and adverse weather scenarios where traditional methods fail.

**Failure Signatures:** Poor reconstruction quality indicates issues in the preprocessing stage, particularly in emissivity estimation or temperature extraction. Visual artifacts may appear when material properties are incorrectly mapped to HSV channels or when the NeRF network fails to properly condition on the physical property inputs.
