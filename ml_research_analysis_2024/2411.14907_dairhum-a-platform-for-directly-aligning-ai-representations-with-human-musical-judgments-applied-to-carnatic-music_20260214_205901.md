# DAIRHuM: A Platform for Directly Aligning AI Representations with Human Musical Judgments applied to Carnatic Music

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2411.14907
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.14907
- Reference count: 10
- One-line result: Platform achieves 66.66% alignment between human expert rhythmic judgments and NSynth model embeddings for Carnatic percussion duets

## Executive Summary
DAIRHuM introduces a novel platform for directly aligning AI model representations with human musical judgments, specifically designed for Carnatic music analysis. The system enables musicians and researchers to collect human similarity judgments through an intuitive web interface while simultaneously assessing pre-trained model alignment using quantitative scores and visual plots. Applied to NSynth embeddings of Carnatic rhythmic duets, the platform reveals significant findings about model alignment with expert human judgments of rhythmic harmony, demonstrating both the platform's utility and highlighting genre-specific challenges in AI music analysis.

## Method Summary
The DAIRHuM platform implements a multi-component architecture where human participants label similarity judgments through an interactive interface while AI model representations are simultaneously evaluated. The system processes audio recordings, extracts pre-trained model embeddings (specifically NSynth in this case), and presents both human-labeled data and model outputs for comparison. Quantitative alignment scores are computed alongside visual plots to facilitate comprehensive analysis of how well AI representations match human musical perception. The platform's design emphasizes accessibility for both musicians and experimentalists, enabling direct comparison between human and AI assessments of musical relationships.

## Key Results
- Achieved 66.66% alignment score between human expert rhythmic judgments and NSynth model assessments
- Revealed significant differences in rhythm perception specific to Carnatic music traditions
- Demonstrated platform's effectiveness in making music AI tools accessible for culturally intricate musical traditions

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The platform works by creating a direct interface between human perceptual judgments and AI model representations, allowing for systematic comparison and alignment assessment. By presenting musical excerpts to human participants for similarity labeling while simultaneously processing the same material through pre-trained models, DAIRHuM establishes a measurable framework for evaluating AI-human alignment. The quantitative scoring and visual plotting capabilities provide clear feedback on alignment quality, enabling researchers to identify where and why AI models succeed or fail in capturing human musical understanding, particularly in the nuanced context of Carnatic percussion.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map:** Web interface -> Human judgment collection -> Audio processing -> Model embedding extraction -> Alignment computation -> Visualization

**Critical Path:** Audio recording → Model embedding extraction → Human similarity judgment → Alignment score calculation → Visual analysis

**Design Tradeoffs:** The platform prioritizes accessibility and usability for non-technical musicians over advanced customization options, potentially limiting flexibility for specialized research needs. The choice of NSynth as the pre-trained model simplifies implementation but may not capture all relevant musical features for Carnatic music analysis.

**Failure Signatures:** Low alignment scores indicate either fundamental mismatches between human perception and model representations, or issues with the specific model's ability to capture genre-specific musical features. Inconsistent human judgments may suggest ambiguous musical relationships or cultural differences in interpretation.
