# Document-level Clinical Entity and Relation Extraction via Knowledge Base-Guided Generation

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2407.10021
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.10021
- Reference count: 15
- Primary result: GPT-4-32k with UMLS integration achieves 80% F1 on n2c2 and 94% on ADE datasets

## Executive Summary
This paper presents a novel approach for document-level clinical entity and relation extraction that integrates UMLS knowledge base concepts with large language models. The method uses MetaMap to map UMLS concepts to clinical text, then incorporates these concepts into few-shot prompts for GPT models. The approach significantly improves performance on clinical entity recognition and relation extraction tasks compared to standard language models and RAG-based approaches.

## Method Summary
The method leverages MetaMap to identify UMLS concepts in clinical text, which are then incorporated into prompts for few-shot learning with GPT models. This knowledge base-guided generation approach combines the strengths of structured medical knowledge with the reasoning capabilities of large language models. The system processes clinical documents to extract entities and their relationships while benefiting from the semantic richness provided by UMLS integration.

## Key Results
- GPT-4-32k with UMLS integration achieves 80% F1 score on n2c2 dataset
- GPT-4-32k with UMLS integration achieves 94% F1 score on ADE dataset
- Outperforms standard GPT models by 4-12% and RAG-based approaches
- Demonstrates improved identification of medication-related entities and relationships

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The method works by augmenting language model prompts with structured medical knowledge from UMLS. MetaMap identifies relevant medical concepts in clinical text, providing semantic context that helps the language model better understand relationships between entities. This knowledge-guided approach reduces ambiguity in clinical language and improves the model's ability to recognize complex medication-related entities and their relationships within documents.

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**: Clinical text -> MetaMap -> UMLS concepts -> Prompt engineering -> GPT-4-32k -> Entity/relation extraction

**Critical Path**: The bottleneck occurs at MetaMap concept extraction, which must efficiently process clinical text to generate relevant UMLS concepts. GPT-4-32k then uses these concepts in prompts for few-shot learning to extract entities and relations.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The approach trades computational overhead of MetaMap processing for improved extraction accuracy. The 32k context window of GPT-4 is leveraged to maintain document-level context while incorporating knowledge base concepts.

**Failure Signatures**: Performance degradation may occur when MetaMap fails to map domain-specific terminology or when UMLS concepts are too general to provide useful context. Over-reliance on knowledge base concepts could also lead to missing novel entity types.
