# GenesisTex2: Stable, Consistent and High-Quality Text-to-Texture Generation

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2409.18401
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.18401
- Authors: Jiawei Lu; Yingpeng Zhang; Zengjun Zhao; He Wang; Kun Zhou; Tianjia Shao
- Reference count: 21
- Primary result: Novel text-to-texture synthesis framework leveraging pretrained diffusion models with local attention reweighing and latent space merge pipeline for improved consistency and quality

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces GenesisTex2, a text-to-texture synthesis framework that builds upon pretrained diffusion models like Stable Diffusion. The method introduces a local attention reweighing mechanism to focus on spatially correlated patches across different views, enhancing local details while preserving cross-view consistency. Additionally, it proposes a latent space merge pipeline that ensures consistency across viewpoints without sacrificing diversity. The approach does not require additional training or fine-tuning, making it highly adaptable to various models.

## Method Summary
GenesisTex2 leverages pretrained diffusion models through a novel local attention reweighing mechanism in self-attention layers. This mechanism calculates weights based on 3D distances between patches across different views, allowing the model to concentrate on spatially correlated regions. The method also introduces a latent space merge pipeline that uses view-dependent merging weights based on cosine similarity between view normals and canonical view direction. Finally, an efficient surface space color dilation algorithm fills occluded regions by propagating colors based on 3D proximity and surface normal similarity. The framework operates without requiring additional training or fine-tuning of the underlying diffusion model.

## Key Results
- Significantly outperforms existing techniques in texture consistency and visual quality
- Faster than distillation-based methods while maintaining high fidelity
- Achieves strong results across multiple metrics including FID/KID scores and user study ratings

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1: Local Attention Reweighing
- Claim: Cross-view local attention improves multi-view consistency by focusing on spatially correlated patches across views
- Core assumption: 3D geometry provides reliable spatial correspondence between patches across different views
- Evidence: Weight matrix calculated based on 3D distances between patches, with closer patches receiving higher weights
- Break condition: Inaccurate 3D geometry or poor UV parameterization

### Mechanism 2: Latent Space Merge Pipeline
- Claim: View-dependent merging preserves high-frequency details while ensuring consistency across views
- Core assumption: Merging weights based on view normal similarity can maintain diversity while ensuring consistency
- Evidence: Denoised predictions merged using weights based on cosine similarity between view normals
- Break condition: Poorly calibrated view-dependent weights leading to inconsistency or loss of diversity

### Mechanism 3: Surface Space Color Dilation
- Claim: Propagating colors based on 3D proximity and surface normal similarity produces more geometrically consistent results than UV-based methods
- Core assumption: Surface space approximation can efficiently fill occluded regions while maintaining geometric consistency
- Evidence: Algorithm divides UV map into sub-islands and propagates colors based on 3D distance and normal similarity
- Break condition: Complex geometry with many occluded regions overwhelming the surface space approximation

## Architecture Onboarding

- Component map: Input (3D mesh, text prompt) -> Local attention mechanism -> Latent merge pipeline -> Surface space dilation -> Output (texture map)
- Critical path:
  1. Render position maps and calculate attention weights
  2. Apply local attention modification during diffusion
  3. Perform latent merge at each denoising step
  4. Decode and merge final images
  5. Apply surface space dilation to fill occluded regions

- Design tradeoffs:
  - Local vs global attention: Local attention preserves details but requires accurate 3D geometry
  - View count: More views improve consistency but increase computation time
  - Merging strategy: View-dependent merging preserves diversity but adds complexity

- Failure signatures:
  - Inconsistent textures across views: Likely due to inaccurate 3D geometry or poor attention weight calculation
  - Loss of detail or diversity: May indicate over-aggressive merging or incorrect view weighting
  - Artifacts in occluded regions: Suggests issues with the surface space dilation algorithm

- First 3 experiments:
  1. Test local attention with synthetic geometry where ground truth correspondences are known
  2. Compare view-dependent merging with uniform averaging on simple shapes
  3. Evaluate surface space dilation on meshes with known occlusion patterns

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out

### Open Question 1
- Question: How does the local attention mechanism perform on meshes with highly irregular or non-manifold geometry?
- Basis: Method relies on distance calculations between patches in 3D space, assuming well-defined and manifold mesh
- Why unresolved: Paper does not evaluate on meshes with irregular or non-manifold geometry
- What evidence would resolve it: Testing on dataset of meshes with varying geometric complexity and non-manifold features

### Open Question 2
- Question: What is the impact of grid size parameter in surface space color dilation algorithm?
- Basis: Paper mentions grid size parameter but does not discuss its impact or sensitivity
- Why unresolved: No ablation study or sensitivity analysis on grid size parameter
- What evidence would resolve it: Experiments with varying grid sizes analyzing resulting textures for artifacts and visual quality

### Open Question 3
- Question: How does the method handle textures with high-frequency details or fine patterns?
- Basis: Paper mentions preserving local details but does not specifically address high-frequency details
- Why unresolved: No examples or analysis of textures with high-frequency details
- What evidence would resolve it: Testing on textures with known high-frequency details and evaluating preservation

## Limitations

- Effectiveness heavily depends on accuracy of 3D geometry and UV parameterization
- Surface space dilation algorithm is an approximation that may struggle with highly occluded regions
- Performance on diverse 3D mesh types (non-triangular, non-manifold, or with discontinuities) remains untested

## Confidence

**High Confidence:**
- Overall framework architecture and pipeline are well-defined and technically sound
- Improvement in texture consistency metrics (FID/KID scores) is empirically validated
- Speed advantage over distillation-based methods is measurable and significant

**Medium Confidence:**
- Effectiveness of local attention reweighing mechanism in preserving cross-view consistency
- Ability of latent merge pipeline to maintain diversity while ensuring consistency
- Efficiency of surface space dilation algorithm compared to traditional UV-based methods

**Low Confidence:**
- Generalization to diverse 3D mesh types beyond tested categories
- Performance on highly complex geometries with significant occlusion
- Scalability for real-time or production applications

## Next Checks

1. **Geometry Robustness Test:** Evaluate performance on meshes with known UV parameterization issues (non-manifold geometry, texture seams, discontinuities) and compare consistency metrics and visual quality against ground truth textures.

2. **Occlusion Stress Test:** Create synthetic meshes with controlled occlusion patterns and evaluate surface space dilation algorithm's ability to fill occluded regions accurately, measuring geometric consistency using surface normal deviation and color propagation error.

3. **Scalability Benchmark:** Measure computational overhead (memory usage, inference time) as number of input views increases, comparing performance on high-resolution meshes (>100K faces) and assessing trade-off between view count and texture quality.
