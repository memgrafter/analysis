## Executive Summary
This survey provides a comprehensive overview of deep learning methods for audio generation, covering three key aspects: audio representations, model architectures, and evaluation metrics. It traces the evolution from rule-based systems to modern deep learning approaches, highlighting the shift from domain-specific knowledge to general-purpose methods. The survey explains five major deep learning architectures used in audio generation—autoencoders, generative adversarial networks, normalizing flows, transformer networks, and diffusion models—along with their extensions and applications. It also reviews four evaluation metrics commonly used in audio generation tasks. The work serves as a foundational resource for researchers entering the field, offering insights into current state-of-the-art methods and relevant studies for future research.

## Architecture Onboarding

Component Map:
Audio Data -> Preprocessing (Resampling, Normalization) -> Feature Extraction (Spectrograms, Mel-spectrograms) -> Deep Learning Model (AE, GAN, NF, Transformer, Diffusion) -> Postprocessing (Inversion, Denoising) -> Generated Audio Output

Critical Path:
Preprocessing → Feature Extraction → Model Architecture → Training → Inference → Postprocessing

Design Tradeoffs:
- Computational complexity vs. audio quality
- Real-time generation capability vs. model expressiveness
- Representation detail level vs. processing efficiency
- Training stability vs. model capacity

Failure Signatures:
- Mode collapse in GANs producing repetitive audio patterns
- Over-smoothing in autoencoders losing audio detail
- Training instability in diffusion models
- Loss of temporal coherence in transformer-based approaches
