## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of improving job matching systems at LinkedIn by enhancing the retrieval step of a two-stage search and recommendation paradigm. The authors propose a learning-to-retrieve approach that leverages both graph-based auto-targeting for promoted jobs and embedding-based retrieval with quality rules for organic jobs. The system aims to improve both the efficiency of promoted job targeting and the personalization of organic job recommendations.

The key innovation lies in the development of a novel on-GPU solution that supports both KNN and term matching efficiently, outperforming traditional inverted index-based systems. This approach allows for more effective and scalable job retrieval, addressing the limitations of existing methods that either struggle with recall or fail to leverage personalized embeddings effectively.

## Method Summary
The proposed method separates the retrieval process for promoted and organic jobs, recognizing their distinct requirements and challenges. For promoted jobs, the system constructs a graph using confirmed hire data to establish links between job seekers and job segments, enabling efficient auto-targeting. This graph-based approach allows for more precise targeting of job advertisements, potentially improving budget utilization for employers.

For organic jobs, the system employs embedding-based retrieval with negative sampling strategies and curriculum learning. This approach aims to improve the personalization of job recommendations by learning embeddings that capture the relevance between job seekers and job postings. The system also incorporates quality rules to ensure that retrieved results meet certain standards of relevance and quality.

A key innovation is the development of an on-GPU solution that efficiently supports both KNN and term matching. This solution outperforms traditional inverted index-based systems by leveraging the parallel processing capabilities of GPUs, enabling faster and more scalable retrieval operations.

## Key Results
- 15% increase in budget utilization for promoted jobs through graph-based auto-targeting
- Significant gains in user engagement for organic jobs using embedding-based retrieval
- Novel on-GPU solution outperforms traditional inverted index-based systems in efficiency and scalability

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The proposed approach works by addressing the specific challenges of job matching in a large-scale professional network. The graph-based auto-targeting for promoted jobs leverages the rich graph structure of LinkedIn's professional network, using confirmed hire data to establish meaningful connections between job seekers and job segments. This allows for more precise targeting of job advertisements, potentially improving the efficiency of ad spend for employers.

The embedding-based retrieval for organic jobs captures the complex relationships between job seekers and job postings in a low-dimensional space. By incorporating negative sampling strategies and curriculum learning, the system learns more discriminative embeddings that can better distinguish between relevant and irrelevant job recommendations. This leads to improved personalization and relevance of organic job suggestions.

The on-GPU solution addresses the computational challenges of large-scale retrieval operations. By leveraging the parallel processing capabilities of GPUs, the system can perform both KNN and term matching operations more efficiently than traditional CPU-based approaches. This enables faster response times and better scalability, crucial for handling the massive scale of LinkedIn's job matching system.

## Limitations
- Evaluation primarily relies on offline metrics and A/B testing results with limited details about statistical significance
- Embedding-based retrieval system details, including negative sampling strategies and curriculum learning parameters, are not fully disclosed
- On-GPU solution may face scalability challenges when applied to other domains or at larger scales
- Business impact claims are based on LinkedIn's internal metrics and have not been independently verified
- Graph-based auto-targeting relies heavily on confirmed hire data, which may not be available in all job matching contexts and could introduce bias

## Confidence
- **High confidence**: The general framework of separating promoted and organic job retrieval, and the description of the on-GPU solution implementation
- **Medium confidence**: The claimed improvements in business metrics and user engagement, given the lack of detailed statistical analysis
- **Low confidence**: The generalizability of the approach to other job matching platforms or domains without confirmed hire data
