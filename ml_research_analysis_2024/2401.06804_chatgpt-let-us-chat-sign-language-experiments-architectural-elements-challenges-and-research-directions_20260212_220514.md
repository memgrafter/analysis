# ChatGPT, Let us Chat Sign Language: Experiments, Architectural Elements, Challenges and Research Directions

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2401.06804
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.06804
- Reference count: 24
- Key outcome: ChatGPT achieves 100% accuracy translating from English to ASL/AUSLAN/BSL and from ArSL to English, but fails with Arabic-to-sign and sign-to-Arabic translations (0% accuracy).

## Executive Summary
This paper investigates ChatGPT's potential for Sign Language Translation (SLT) through experiments translating between English, Arabic, and three sign languages (ASL, AUSLAN, BSL, ArSL) using medical statements. The study reveals that ChatGPT successfully translates from English to ASL, AUSLAN, and BSL with perfect accuracy, and from Arabic Sign Language to English, but fails to translate from Arabic to ArSL or from any sign language to Arabic. The findings suggest that ChatGPT's performance in SLT depends heavily on the availability of training data and the language pair involved, with English-based translations showing superior results compared to Arabic-based ones.

## Method Summary
The study evaluates ChatGPT's translation capabilities using 5 medical-related statements in English and Arabic. The experiments test translations across multiple language pairs: English-AABSL, English-ArSL, Arabic-AABSL, Arabic-ArSL, and their reverse directions. For each translation, ChatGPT is prompted to translate each statement, with up to 4 iterations allowed if the initial attempt is incorrect. Accuracy is measured as the percentage of correct translations achieved within the iteration limit, calculated per statement and averaged across experiments.

## Key Results
- ChatGPT achieves 100% accuracy translating from English to ASL, AUSLAN, and BSL
- ChatGPT achieves 100% accuracy translating from AABSL to English
- ChatGPT fails to translate from Arabic to ArSL with 0% accuracy
- ChatGPT achieves only 27% accuracy translating from Arabic to AABSL

- Mechanism: The model's performance in translating from English to sign languages is high because there is likely more online content and teaching material available for sign languages like ASL, AUSLAN, and BSL. Conversely, the low accuracy in translating to ArSL is attributed to the lack of comprehensive ArSL dictionary and grammar resources.
- Core assumption: The model's training data includes sufficient and diverse resources for the languages it translates accurately.

- Core assumption: The model's architecture and training are optimized for text-based language tasks.

## Architecture Onboarding

- Component map: Input text -> Encoder -> Transformer layers with attention -> Decoder-only transformer -> Output text
- Critical path: The critical path for translation involves encoding the input sequence, processing it through the transformer layers, and generating the output sequence token by token.
- Design tradeoffs: The use of a decoder-only transformer allows for efficient handling of long sequences but may limit the model's ability to generate complex sign language representations. The trade-off between model size and energy consumption is also significant, as larger models require more computational resources.
- Failure signatures: Common failure modes include incorrect translations due to insufficient training data for certain languages, inability to handle complex grammatical structures, and poor performance in translating from spoken to sign languages.
- First 3 experiments:
  1. Translate a simple English sentence to ASL to test basic translation capabilities.
  2. Translate a complex medical statement from English to ArSL to evaluate domain-specific translation accuracy.
  3. Translate a sign language sentence from AABSL to English to assess the model's ability to understand and generate sign language representations.

## Limitations
- Small sample size of only 5 medical statements limits generalizability to broader translation tasks.
- Lack of detailed information about exact sign language gloss conventions used in experiments.
- Focus on medical statements may not represent the full complexity of general translation tasks.

## Confidence

- ChatGPT achieves 100% accuracy translating from AABSL to English
- ChatGPT fails to translate from Arabic to ArSL with 0% accuracy

**Medium Confidence Claims:**

- ChatGPT performs better with English than Arabic across all translation directions

**Low Confidence Claims:**

- The generalizability of results to non-medical translation tasks

## Next Checks

1. **Reproduce with expanded dataset**: Conduct the same experiments using 50-100 medical and non-medical statements across all language pairs to verify the consistency of reported accuracy rates and assess generalizability.

2. **Expert linguistic validation**: Engage certified sign language interpreters to independently evaluate the correctness of ChatGPT's sign language translations, particularly for AABSL and ArSL, to verify the claimed 0% and 27% accuracy rates.

3. **Ablation study on training data**: Test ChatGPT's performance on translating between languages for which it was explicitly trained versus those where it had limited exposure, to empirically validate the hypothesis that translation accuracy correlates with training data availability.
