# Benchmarking of LLM Detection: Comparing Two Competing Approaches

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID:** 2406.11670
- **Source URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.11670
- **Reference count:** 7
- **Primary result:** GPTZero achieves 97.45% accuracy, 94.90% precision, and 100% recall on Amazon phone review detection task, significantly outperforming ZeroGPT

## Executive Summary
This study benchmarks two LLM text detectors, GPTZero and ZeroGPT, using a newly created evaluation dataset of 1000 human-written and 1000 AI-generated Amazon phone reviews. The evaluation reveals significant performance differences between the detectors, with GPTZero demonstrating superior accuracy (97.45% vs 84.05%), precision (94.90% vs 73.20%), and recall (100% vs 93.49%). Notably, GPTZero achieved zero false negatives, correctly identifying all AI-generated texts. The results demonstrate that current detection approaches vary substantially in effectiveness and highlight the importance of robust evaluation methodologies.

## Method Summary
The authors created a balanced evaluation dataset by collecting 1000 Amazon phone reviews written by humans and generating 1000 reviews using GPT-4 with carefully crafted prompts. Both GPTZero and ZeroGPT were then applied to this dataset to classify each review as human-written or AI-generated. Performance metrics including accuracy, precision, and recall were calculated for each detector. The study focused on a single text domain (product reviews) and used a binary classification setup to assess detection capabilities.

## Key Results
- GPTZero achieved 97.45% accuracy compared to ZeroGPT's 84.05% accuracy
- GPTZero demonstrated 94.90% precision versus ZeroGPT's 73.20% precision
- GPTZero achieved perfect recall (100%) with zero false negatives, while ZeroGPT had 93.49% recall

## Limitations
- Evaluation limited to Amazon phone reviews only, restricting generalizability to other domains
- Single dataset size (1000 human, 1000 AI reviews) provides limited statistical power
- No cross-validation or multiple random splits raises overfitting concerns
- Binary classification setup may not capture nuanced detection challenges
- Impact of text length and language variation on detection performance unexamined

## Confidence
**High confidence:**
- GPTZero demonstrates superior performance metrics compared to ZeroGPT on the tested dataset, with statistically meaningful differences in accuracy, precision, and recall
- The zero false negative rate for GPTZero is a notable and reliable finding for this specific evaluation

**Medium confidence:**
- The superior performance of GPTZero will generalize to other text domains and detector types
- The dataset construction methodology adequately represents real-world challenges in LLM detection
