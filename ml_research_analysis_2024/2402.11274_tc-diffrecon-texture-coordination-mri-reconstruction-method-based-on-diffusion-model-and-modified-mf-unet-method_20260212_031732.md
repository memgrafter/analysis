# TC-DiffRecon: Texture coordination MRI reconstruction method based on diffusion model and modified MF-UNet method

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2402.11274
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.11274
- Reference count: 0
- Outperforms state-of-the-art methods on FastMRI dataset with superior PSNR and SSIM metrics across varying acceleration factors

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces TC-DiffRecon, a diffusion model-based MRI reconstruction method that addresses key limitations in existing approaches, including image fragmentation, incoherence, and excessive smoothing. The method combines three novel components: Multi-Free U-Net (MF-UNet) for dynamic feature balancing, Texture Coordination K-space Guidance (TCKG) for data consistency and texture harmonization, and Coarse-to-Fine (C2F) sampling for accelerated reconstruction. Experimental results on the FastMRI dataset demonstrate significant improvements over state-of-the-art methods, achieving better image quality metrics while maintaining computational efficiency.

## Method Summary
TC-DiffRecon is built on a diffusion model framework that generates high-quality MRI images from undersampled K-space data. The core innovation lies in the MF-UNet module, which dynamically balances backbone and skip connection features using scaling factors to prevent over-smoothing while maintaining denoising effectiveness. The TCKG module ensures data consistency with K-space constraints while preventing texture incoherence through iterative noise reapplication and denoising. The C2F sampling scheme accelerates the reconstruction process by compressing full sampling steps into shorter schedules and averaging multiple parallel samples. Together, these components enable efficient and high-quality MRI reconstruction across various acceleration factors.

## Key Results
- Achieves superior PSNR and SSIM metrics compared to state-of-the-art methods on FastMRI dataset
- Effectively addresses image fragmentation, incoherence, and excessive smoothing issues
- Demonstrates consistent performance improvements across varying acceleration factors
- Maintains computational efficiency through the Coarse-to-Fine sampling scheme

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: The MF-UNet dynamically balances backbone and skip connection features to improve denoising without excessive smoothing.
- Mechanism: The model introduces two scaling factors—one amplifies backbone features (αl) and the other attenuates skip connection features (sl). This selective modulation allows the backbone to focus on denoising while preventing skip connections from introducing high-frequency noise that causes over-smoothing.
- Core assumption: U-Net's original skip connections contribute to high-frequency noise that degrades image quality in diffusion models.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "The MF-UNet incorporates two uniquely designed modulation factors, which dynamically balance the contributions from the trunk and jump-connect features intrinsic to the U-Net architecture."
  - [section 2.2] "To harmonize these feature mappings, our MF-UNet employs two scalar factors... The first factor, known as the backbone feature factor, is capable of amplifying the primary backbone features, thereby enhancing the denoising process."
  - [corpus] Weak. No direct corpus evidence about U-Net feature balancing in diffusion models.
- Break condition: If the scaling factors become imbalanced (e.g., αl too large), the model may over-amplify noise instead of suppressing it.

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: The TCKG module ensures data consistency while preventing texture incoherence during K-space integration.
- Mechanism: The module iteratively applies noise to the denoised output and re-runs the denoising process multiple times (K iterations), which smooths out texture inconsistencies introduced by direct K-space projection.
- Core assumption: Direct K-space data consistency operations can introduce textural discordance between the generated image and the K-space constraints.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "the TCKG module ensures data consistency and image texture harmonization by iteratively integrating K-space information."
  - [section 2.3.2] "We propose a texture coordination strategy: noise is reapplied to the yt−1 generated at step t, creating a new yt. Subsequently, denoising at step t is repeated K times."
  - [corpus] Weak. No direct corpus evidence of iterative texture coordination in diffusion models.
- Break condition: If K is set too low, texture incoherence persists; if too high, the model may converge to a suboptimal solution.

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: The Coarse-to-Fine sampling scheme accelerates the sampling process while reducing texture incoherence.
- Mechanism: The model compresses the full sampling steps (T) into shorter, uniformly spaced schedules (T/k), averages multiple parallel samples, and then refines the result with additional steps to remove ambiguity.
- Core assumption: Averaging multiple shorter-schedule samples reduces noise and texture artifacts compared to full-step sampling.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "the Coarse-to-Fine (C2F) sampling scheme further accelerates the sampling process and reduces texture incoherence."
  - [section 2.4] "We accomplish this by compressing the T-step sampling process to uniformly spaced shorter schedules T, T-k, ..., 1... The results of the noise are averaged to obtain yavg 0."
  - [corpus] Weak. No direct corpus evidence of C2F sampling in diffusion models for MRI.
- Break condition: If k is too large, the model may skip critical denoising steps, leading to poor image quality.

## Architecture Onboarding

- Component map:
  - Undersampled K-space data (xobs) -> MF-UNet -> TCKG -> C2F sampling -> Reconstructed MRI image

- Critical path:
  1. Input: Undersampled K-space data (xobs).
  2. MF-UNet predicts noise at step t-1.
  3. TCKG applies iterative texture coordination and data consistency.
  4. C2F sampling accelerates and refines the output.

- Design tradeoffs:
  - MF-UNet: Balances denoising and texture preservation but may introduce computational overhead.
  - TCKG: Ensures data consistency but may slow down the process due to iterative steps.
  - C2F sampling: Accelerates sampling but may sacrifice some detail if k is too large.

- Failure signatures:
  - Excessive smoothing: Indicates MF-UNet scaling factors are imbalanced.
  - Texture incoherence: Suggests TCKG iterations are insufficient or improperly configured.
  - Slow sampling: Points to C2F parameters (k) being too conservative.

- First 3 experiments:
  1. Test MF-UNet with varying backbone and skip scaling factors to find the optimal balance.
  2. Evaluate TCKG with different numbers of texture coordination iterations (K) to minimize incoherence.
  3. Compare C2F sampling with different k values to balance speed and image quality.
