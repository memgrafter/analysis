# Transforming Norm-based To Graph-based Spatial Representation for Spatio-Temporal Epidemiological Models

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2402.14539
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.14539
- **Reference count**: 40
- **Primary result**: Framework converts norm-based spatial representations to graph-based representations with 94% average accuracy preservation

## Executive Summary
This paper presents a framework for converting norm-based spatial representations to graph-based representations in epidemiological modeling. The approach uses agent-based simulations combined with heuristic optimization algorithms to approximate graph node locations and population spatial walk dynamics. The method was evaluated across three real-world cases (airport, restaurant, bus) and synthetic cases, achieving an average accuracy preservation of 94%. The framework demonstrates relative robustness to changes in both spatial and temporal properties, with the Time Series X-means (TSxM) algorithm combined with Multi-agent Classification with AutoEncoder (MAC-AE) showing the most consistent performance across different scenarios.

## Method Summary
The framework converts norm-based spatial representations to graph-based representations using agent-based simulations and heuristic algorithms. It employs graph search algorithms (Quadtree, GA, TSxM, RNN) to identify graph nodes and walk dynamics approximation algorithms (MC, MAC-AE, DRL) to approximate movement patterns between nodes. The framework uses a spatial partitioning approach where population distribution and spatial dynamics are represented as geometrical circles, which are then approximated using graph-based spatial representation. The accuracy preservation is evaluated by comparing the results of the graph-based representation with the original norm-based representation across multiple scenarios.

## Key Results
- Achieved 94% average accuracy preservation when converting norm-based to graph-based representations
- TSxM combined with MAC-AE consistently outperformed other algorithm combinations across both synthetic and real-world cases
- Framework demonstrated relative robustness to changes in spatial and temporal properties
- Graph-based representations offered computational efficiency while maintaining model expressiveness

## Architecture Onboarding

- **Component map**: Norm-based spatial representation -> Graph search algorithm (Quadtree/GA/TSxM/RNN) -> Walk dynamics approximation (MC/MAC-AE/DRL) -> Graph-based spatial representation with approximated walk dynamics

- **Critical path**:
  1. Convert norm-based spatial representation to graph-based representation using graph search algorithm
  2. Approximate walk dynamics between graph nodes using walk dynamics approximation algorithm
  3. Simulate agent movements and interactions in graph-based representation
  4. Compare results with norm-based representation to evaluate accuracy preservation

- **Design tradeoffs**:
  - Computational efficiency vs. accuracy preservation: Graph-based representations are more computationally efficient but may sacrifice some accuracy compared to norm-based representations
  - Algorithm selection: Different combinations of graph search and walk dynamics approximation algorithms may be better suited for different types of epidemiological models and spatial configurations
  - Sensitivity to changes in spatial and temporal properties: The framework's performance may degrade for specific combinations of spatial and temporal properties

- **Failure signatures**:
  - Significant loss of accuracy compared to norm-based representation
  - Computational efficiency gains not justifying the approximation
  - Poor performance for specific types of epidemiological models or spatial configurations
  - Sensitivity to changes in spatial and temporal properties not captured in sensitivity analysis

- **First 3 experiments**:
  1. Implement and test the Quadtree algorithm for graph search on a simple synthetic case with a single circle and random walk dynamics
  2. Implement and test the TSxM algorithm for graph search combined with the MAC-AE algorithm for walk dynamics approximation on a more complex synthetic case with multiple circles and different population densities
  3. Implement and test the framework on a real-world case, such as the airport scenario, and compare the results with the norm-based representation to evaluate accuracy preservation and computational efficiency gains

## Limitations

- Computational efficiency claims lack detailed analysis of runtime and memory usage across different scales
- Framework's performance on heterogeneous populations (varying ages, health conditions, mobility patterns) remains unexplored
- Validation cases, while including real-world scenarios, are limited in number and may not capture the full diversity of epidemiological contexts

## Confidence

- **High**: The core mechanism of using agent-based simulations to approximate graph-based representations is well-supported by the experimental results across multiple cases.
- **Medium**: The claim about the TSxM + MAC-AE combination being the best performer is supported by the data but requires further validation across more diverse scenarios.
- **Low**: The computational efficiency claims lack quantitative support, and the framework's behavior with heterogeneous populations and different disease types remains largely theoretical.
