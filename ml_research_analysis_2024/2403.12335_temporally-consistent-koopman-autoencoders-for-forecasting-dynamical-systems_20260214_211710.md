# Temporally Consistent Koopman Autoencoders for Forecasting Dynamical Systems

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2403.12335
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.12335
- Authors: Indranil Nayak; Ananda Chakrabarty; Mrinal Kumar; Fernando Teixeira; Debdipta Goswami
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: tcKAE improves long-term forecasting of high-dimensional dynamical systems using limited and noisy data

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces the Temporally-Consistent Koopman Autoencoder (tcKAE), a novel approach to improve long-term forecasting of high-dimensional dynamical systems using limited and noisy training data. The key innovation is a consistency regularization term that enforces temporal coherence among predictions in the Koopman invariant latent space, enhancing model robustness and generalizability. The tcKAE leverages the linearity of the Koopman operator in an infinite-dimensional function space to learn a reduced-order feature space with simpler dynamics.

## Method Summary
The tcKAE method builds upon the Koopman Autoencoder (KAE) framework, which uses the linearity of the Koopman operator to learn a reduced-order feature space. The key addition is a consistency regularization term that enforces temporal coherence among predictions in the Koopman invariant latent space. This term encourages the model to make predictions that are consistent with the temporal evolution of the system, improving robustness and generalizability, especially for limited and noisy training data. The method is evaluated on four datasets: an undamped pendulum, an oscillating electron beam, flow past a cylinder, and sea surface temperature data.

## Key Results
- tcKAE outperforms state-of-the-art KAE models, particularly for shorter training lengths and noisy data
- On the pendulum dataset with 32 training samples and 40 dB SNR, tcKAE achieved a relative 2-norm error of 4.018% compared to 9.684% for the baseline cKAE
- The method shows promise for applications requiring accurate long-term predictions from limited data, such as modeling complex physical systems where simulations are computationally expensive

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The tcKAE leverages the linearity of the Koopman operator in an infinite-dimensional function space to learn a reduced-order feature space with simpler dynamics. By imposing temporal consistency constraints, the method reduces sensitivity to noise and improves prediction accuracy, especially for limited datasets. The consistency regularization term enforces temporal coherence among predictions in the Koopman invariant latent space, which enhances model robustness and generalizability.

## Limitations
- The evaluation is limited to four relatively simple dynamical systems, which may not fully represent the complexity of real-world applications
- The analytical justification based on Koopman spectral theory may not capture all practical challenges in implementing tcKAE on complex, high-dimensional systems
- The paper does not discuss computational efficiency or scalability of the method, which could be critical for real-world applications

## Confidence
High confidence in the theoretical framework and the proposed consistency regularization term. Medium confidence in the performance claims, as they are based on a limited set of datasets. Low confidence in the scalability and applicability to real-world, complex dynamical systems without further validation.
