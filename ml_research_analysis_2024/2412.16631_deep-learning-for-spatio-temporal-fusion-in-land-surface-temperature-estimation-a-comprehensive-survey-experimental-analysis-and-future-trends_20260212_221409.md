# Deep Learning for Spatio-Temporal Fusion in Land Surface Temperature Estimation: A Comprehensive Survey, Experimental Analysis, and Future Trends

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2412.16631
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.16631
- Reference count: 40
- Key outcome: DL-based STF methods outperform traditional approaches like ESTARFM, achieving average RMSE values below 3°C for LST estimation

## Executive Summary
This paper provides a comprehensive survey of deep learning-based spatio-temporal fusion (STF) methods for land surface temperature (LST) estimation, addressing the challenge of combining high-spatial/low-temporal resolution (HSLT) and low-spatial/high-temporal resolution (LSHT) satellite data. The study introduces a formal mathematical definition of the thermal fusion task, presents a refined taxonomy of relevant DL architectures, and experimentally evaluates representative methods on a newly curated dataset of 51 MODIS-Landsat LST pairs spanning 2013-2024. Results show that DL-based STF methods outperform traditional approaches like ESTARFM, achieving average RMSE values below 3°C, though performance remains limited when LST exhibits strong spatio-temporal variability.

## Method Summary
The study curates a dataset of 51 paired observations from MODIS Terra (1 km, daily) and Landsat 8 (30 m, 16-day revisit) over Orléans Métropole, France (2013-2024). Four DL-based STF methods (STTFN, EDCSTFN, MLFF-GAN) are implemented and trained on 34 temporal triplets using content-based loss functions, then evaluated on 8 test samples using RMSE, SSIM, PSNR, SAM, CC, and ERGAS metrics. Data preprocessing includes cloud gap filling, spatial resampling to 30 m resolution, and creation of 95×95 patches with 20-pixel stride for training.

## Key Results
- DL-based STF methods outperform traditional approaches like ESTARFM, achieving average RMSE values below 3°C
- Performance remains limited when LST exhibits strong spatio-temporal variability, with errors increasing substantially under challenging thermal conditions
- Architectures originally designed for surface reflectance fusion do not directly transfer to thermal data due to fundamental differences in LST spatio-temporal dynamics

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: Deep learning-based spatio-temporal fusion (STF) models outperform classical methods like ESTARFM for LST estimation when LST exhibits moderate spatio-temporal variability.
- Mechanism: DL models automatically learn non-linear relationships between high-spatial/low-temporal resolution (HSLT) and low-spatial/high-temporal resolution (LSHT) LST data, capturing complex thermal patterns missed by linear weight-based approaches.
- Core assumption: LST spatio-temporal dynamics can be modeled by non-linear functions that generalize across land cover types and weather conditions.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract]: "Results show that DL-based STF methods outperform traditional approaches like ESTARFM, achieving average RMSE values below 3°C"
  - [section 4.1.1]: "Although CNNs are primarily designed for spatial modeling, temporal information can be incorporated by processing multiple temporal images through separate CNN streams"
  - [corpus]: Weak evidence - neighbor papers focus on LST super-resolution but don't provide direct comparative performance data against ESTARFM
- Break condition: LST exhibits strong spatio-temporal variability that violates the assumption of learnable non-linear patterns, or training data lacks sufficient diversity to capture real-world thermal dynamics.

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: DL models fail to accurately reconstruct LST when LST exhibits strong spatio-temporal variability, despite their ability to capture moderate variations.
- Mechanism: Under high thermal variability conditions, the learned non-linear mappings become unstable and overfit to training conditions, leading to large prediction errors and artifacts.
- Core assumption: Training datasets contain representative thermal variability patterns that enable generalization to unseen conditions.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract]: "performance remains limited when LST exhibits strong spatio-temporal variability"
  - [section 5.3]: "For 12 Apr 2024 and 19 Sep 2024, all evaluated models fail to accurately reconstruct the spatio-temporal variability of LST. Errors increase substantially"
  - [section 5.4]: Qualitative analysis shows systematic failures across urban, industrial, and residential subregions under challenging thermal conditions
- Break condition: Training data lacks sufficient thermal variability coverage, or the model architecture cannot capture the full range of LST dynamics present in real-world conditions.

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: STF models originally developed for surface reflectance (SR) do not directly generalize to LST due to fundamental differences in spatio-temporal dynamics.
- Mechanism: LST exhibits rapid temporal fluctuations and sharp spatial discontinuities driven by atmospheric conditions and thermal properties, while SR varies gradually with phenology and illumination changes. Models trained on SR data cannot capture these LST-specific characteristics.
- Core assumption: LST and SR have sufficiently different physical properties that require domain-specific model adaptations.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract]: "LST exhibits rapid temporal variability, often at hourly or sub-hourly scales, driven by atmospheric conditions, surface energy balance, wind, cloud dynamics, and material-dependent thermal properties"
  - [section 2.3.2]: "LST, however, is subject to rapid temporal fluctuations due to diurnal cycles, precipitation events, wind, and other atmospheric conditions"
  - [section 5.3]: "importantly, the relatively small performance gap between traditional and DL-based methods suggests that architectures originally designed for SR fusion do not directly transfer to thermal data"
- Break condition: Models can be successfully adapted through domain-specific modifications, or the fundamental differences between LST and SR are overstated.

## Architecture Onboarding

- Component map: MODIS/Terra LST (1 km, daily) and Landsat 8 LST (30 m, 16-day revisit) -> Preprocessing (gap-filling, resampling) -> Feature extraction (separate CNN streams) -> Fusion layer (combination of spatial and temporal representations) -> Output layer (high-resolution LST prediction) -> Evaluation (RMSE, SSIM, PSNR, SAM, CC, ERGAS)
- Critical path: MODIS-Landsat pair → Preprocessing → Feature extraction → Fusion → Prediction → Evaluation
- Design tradeoffs:
  - Spatial context vs. computational cost: 95×95 patches with 20-pixel stride balance detail preservation and GPU memory
  - Temporal pairs vs. training data availability: Using multiple temporal pairs increases model robustness but requires longer observation periods
  - Linear vs. non-linear modeling: DL captures complex relationships but requires more data and is prone to overfitting
- Failure signatures:
  - Large RMSE values (>3°C) indicate poor LST reconstruction accuracy
  - Block-like artifacts in predictions suggest patch-based processing issues
  - Systematic underestimation or overestimation of LST in specific land cover types indicates model bias
  - Inability to capture sharp thermal gradients near rivers or urban boundaries suggests insufficient spatial modeling
- First 3 experiments:
  1. Baseline comparison: Implement ESTARFM and one DL model on a simple homogeneous ROI to establish performance baseline
  2. Land cover sensitivity: Test models on urban vs. forest vs. agricultural regions to identify land cover-specific weaknesses
  3. Temporal variability test: Evaluate model performance on rapidly changing vs. stable LST conditions to assess temporal generalization

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out

### Open Question 1
- Question: Can deep learning-based STF methods be adapted to explicitly handle cloud-induced gaps in LST data, and what is the optimal training strategy for such adaptation?
- Basis in paper: [explicit] The paper identifies handling cloud-induced gaps as a key limitation and notes that few existing methods incorporate missing pixels during training.
- Why unresolved: While some recent work like MUSTFN demonstrates potential, explicit treatment of cloud gaps within STF frameworks remains largely unexplored.
- What evidence would resolve it: Comparative experiments showing improved performance when STF models are trained with cloud-masked data versus standard gap-filling approaches, validated against ground truth.

### Open Question 2
- Question: How can higher spatial resolution optical data (e.g., Sentinel-2, PlanetScope) be effectively integrated into DL-based STF to improve LST resolution beyond the 30m limitation of Landsat?
- Basis in paper: [explicit] The paper discusses the insufficiency of 30m resolution for UHI analysis and mentions the potential of integrating higher-resolution optical sensors.
- Why unresolved: Current approaches rely on linear assumptions, limiting their ability to capture complex urban thermal processes at finer scales.
- What evidence would resolve it: Development and validation of a DL-based STF model that successfully incorporates high-resolution optical data to produce LST estimates at 10m or higher resolution with quantified accuracy improvements.

### Open Question 3
- Question: What architectural innovations are needed to develop truly joint spatio-temporal DL models for STF that can simultaneously capture both spatial structure and temporal dynamics?
- Basis in paper: [explicit] The paper notes that current STF research relies on models that prioritize either spatial or temporal modeling, rarely both in a unified manner.
- Why unresolved: While some studies explore CNN-LSTM combinations, fully unified DL-based STF architectures remain largely underexplored.
- What evidence would resolve it: Development and experimental validation of a new DL architecture that demonstrably outperforms existing spatial-only and temporal-only approaches in capturing LST spatio-temporal variability.

## Limitations
- DL-based STF methods face significant challenges when LST exhibits strong spatio-temporal variability
- Experimental analysis limited to single geographic region (Orléans Métropole, France) with 51 MODIS-Landsat pairs spanning 2013-2024
- Architectures originally designed for surface reflectance fusion do not directly transfer to thermal data due to fundamental differences in LST spatio-temporal dynamics

## Confidence
- High confidence: LST exhibits rapid temporal variability driven by atmospheric conditions and thermal properties; DL methods achieve lower RMSE than ESTARFM on moderate variability conditions
- Medium confidence: DL architectures designed for SR fusion fail when applied directly to LST due to different physical properties; the newly curated dataset provides a foundation for future research
- Low confidence: The exact reasons for DL model failures under strong spatio-temporal variability are not fully explained; generalizability to other regions and sensors remains untested

## Next Checks
1. **Cross-regional validation**: Test the DL-based STF methods on LST data from multiple geographic regions with diverse land cover types and climate conditions to assess generalizability
2. **Temporal variability analysis**: Systematically evaluate model performance across different LST variability regimes (stable vs. rapidly changing) to quantify the impact of thermal dynamics on fusion accuracy
3. **Architecture adaptation study**: Implement LST-specific modifications to SR-based STF architectures (e.g., incorporating thermal physics constraints) and compare performance against unmodified versions to validate the claim about fundamental differences between LST and SR fusion requirements
