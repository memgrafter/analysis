# GenPalm: Contactless Palmprint Generation with Diffusion Models

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces GenPalm, the first diffusion model-based approach for contactless palmprint generation. The method employs a two-stage process: first generating diverse palm identities using a fine-tuned Stable Diffusion model, then producing multiple images per identity with an identity-preserving ControlNet module. The authors demonstrate that GenPalm generates highly realistic palmprints with useful intra-class and inter-class variations. Experimental results show that recognition models trained on GenPalm-generated synthetic palmprints achieve performance on par with models trained on thousands of real palmprint images.

## Method Summary
GenPalm is a two-stage diffusion model-based framework for generating synthetic contactless palmprints. In the first stage, a fine-tuned Stable Diffusion model generates diverse palm identities by conditioning on textual prompts. The second stage employs a ControlNet module to produce multiple images per identity while preserving identity characteristics. This approach enables the generation of highly realistic palmprints with both intra-class (variations within the same palm) and inter-class (differences between different palms) variations. The method addresses the challenge of limited training data in palmprint recognition systems by providing a scalable solution for synthetic data generation.

## Key Results
- Recognition models trained on GenPalm synthetic palmprints achieve performance comparable to models trained on thousands of real palmprint images
- Models trained on combined real and synthetic data achieve up to 95.67% TAR at 0.01% FAR on cross-database evaluations
- GenPalm successfully generates highly realistic palmprints with useful intra-class and inter-class variations

## Architecture Onboarding

**Component Map**
Stable Diffusion (fine-tuned) -> Identity Generation -> ControlNet -> Multiple Image Generation per Identity

**Critical Path**
The critical path involves first generating diverse palm identities using the fine-tuned Stable Diffusion model, followed by producing multiple images per identity using the ControlNet module. This two-stage approach ensures both diversity and identity preservation in the generated palmprints.

**Design Tradeoffs**
The two-stage generation process introduces complexity but allows for better control over identity preservation and image diversity. Using pre-trained diffusion models (Stable Diffusion and ControlNet) accelerates development but may limit adaptability to specific palmprint characteristics.

**Failure Signatures**
- Poor identity preservation across generated images for the same palm
- Lack of diversity in generated palmprints (limited intra-class variations)
- Unrealistic palmprint features that could mislead recognition models
- Difficulty in generating certain palmprint orientations or poses
