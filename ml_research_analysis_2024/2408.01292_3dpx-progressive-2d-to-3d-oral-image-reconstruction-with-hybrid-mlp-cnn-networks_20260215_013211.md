# 3DPX: Progressive 2D-to-3D Oral Image Reconstruction with Hybrid MLP-CNN Networks

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2408.01292
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.01292
- Reference count: 0
- Primary result: 3DPX achieves SSIM of 74.09, DSC of 63.72, and PSNR of 15.84 on 2D-to-3D dental reconstruction

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces 3DPX, a novel progressive hybrid MLP-CNN pyramid network for reconstructing 3D structures from 2D panoramic X-ray images. The method addresses the challenge of inferring depth-axis spatial information in 2D-to-3D reconstruction, where existing CNN-based approaches are limited by local convolution operations. 3DPX employs a progressive reconstruction strategy with intermediate guidance at each pyramid level and integrates MLPs with CNNs to capture long-range dependencies. Experiments on two large datasets (464 CBCT studies) demonstrate that 3DPX outperforms state-of-the-art methods, achieving SSIM of 74.09, DSC of 63.72, and PSNR of 15.84. The approach also improves downstream angular misalignment classification tasks, showing the effectiveness of the reconstructed 3D structures in enhancing 2D image analysis.

## Method Summary
3DPX is a progressive hybrid MLP-CNN pyramid network that reconstructs 3D dental structures from 2D panoramic X-ray images. The architecture uses a U-Net-like structure with four convolutional blocks in the encoder and four Hybrid MLP-CNN blocks in the decoder. The key innovation is the progressive reconstruction strategy, where intermediate guidance is imposed at each pyramid level through scaled SSE losses. The Hybrid MLP-CNN blocks combine MLPs (for capturing long-range dependencies) with CNNs (for preserving depth coherence), using a 2-step skip connection mechanism. The method processes 128×256 panoramic X-ray inputs to produce 128×256×128 3D flattened structures, with progressive supervision that scales guidance weights at different pyramid levels.

## Key Results
- Achieves SSIM of 74.09, DSC of 63.72, and PSNR of 15.84 on 2D-to-3D reconstruction
- Outperforms state-of-the-art methods by over 6 points in SSIM, 7 points in DSC, and 1.1 points in PSNR
- Improves downstream angular misalignment classification tasks compared to using only 2D input
- Demonstrates superior performance over both pure CNN approaches and residual connection variants

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: Progressive guided reconstruction improves detail capture by enforcing intermediate consistency at each pyramid level.
- Mechanism: The method imposes SSE losses on intermediate decoder outputs at each pyramid level, allowing the network to gradually refine depth information rather than attempting to infer it in a single step.
- Core assumption: Intermediate reconstructions benefit from supervision that scales with proximity to the final output layer.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "We introduce a progressive reconstruction strategy, where 3D images are progressively reconstructed in the 3DPX with guidance imposed on the intermediate reconstruction result at each pyramid level."
  - [section] "Our 3DPX extends the MLP definition with a progressive reconstruction strategy, where 3D images are progressively reconstructed in a pyramid network with guidance imposed on the intermediate reconstruction result at each pyramid level."

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: Hybrid MLP-CNN blocks capture long-range dependencies while preserving depth coherence through skip connections.
- Mechanism: The first convolutional layer fuses features with skip connections before MLP processing, and a second convolutional layer restores depth coherence after MLP processing.
- Core assumption: MLPs can effectively capture long-range dependencies but may disrupt depth channel coherence, which CNNs can restore.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "Further, motivated by the recent advancement of MLPs that show promise in capturing fine-grained long-range dependency, our 3DPX integrates MLPs and CNNs to improve the semantic understanding during reconstruction."
  - [section] "The Hybrid MLP-CNN Block depicted in Fig. 2(c) combines MLP Block proposed by Tu et al [16] called MAXIM and convolutional layer together to benefit from both of their advantages."

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: Progressive guidance provides superior improvement over residual connections in 2D-to-3D reconstruction tasks.
- Mechanism: Unlike residual connections that simply add information, progressive guidance actively shapes intermediate reconstructions through scaled losses, which proves more effective for the complex depth inference problem in 2D-to-3D mapping.
- Core assumption: The depth inference problem requires more than simple residual addition; it needs active correction at multiple scales.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [section] "With progressive intermediate guidance, 3DPX significantly outperformed the U-Net by over 6 points in terms of SSIM, 7 points in terms of DSC, and 1.1 points on PSNR. In contrast, we only observed a moderate improvement with Residual CNN, where SSIM was increased by 2 points."
  - [section] "When progressive guidance sufficiently restored intermediate depth coherence, the residual connection lost its efficacy and failed to surpass the more straightforward designed counterpart."

## Architecture Onboarding

- Component map: Input (128×256 PX) -> Encoder (4 Conv blocks) -> Bottleneck (1 Conv layer) -> Decoder (4 Hybrid MLP-CNN blocks) -> Output (128×256×128 3D structure)

- Critical path: Input → Encoder → Bottleneck → Decoder (with Hybrid MLP-CNN blocks) → Output, with progressive guidance applied throughout decoder.

- Design tradeoffs:
  - Using 2D convolutions instead of 3D preserves depth information in channels but requires careful handling of depth coherence
  - Hybrid MLP-CNN approach captures long-range dependencies but introduces complexity in maintaining spatial consistency
  - Progressive guidance improves reconstruction quality but adds training complexity and computational overhead

- Failure signatures:
  - Blurry or distorted depth structures suggest MLP coherence restoration is failing
  - Inconsistent intermediate reconstructions indicate progressive guidance weights are improperly scaled
  - Poor edge definition may indicate encoder feature extraction is inadequate

- First 3 experiments:
  1. Baseline comparison: Implement a standard U-Net with only 2D convolutions and compare reconstruction quality metrics
  2. Hybrid block ablation: Replace Hybrid MLP-CNN blocks with pure MLP blocks and measure impact on depth coherence
  3. Progressive guidance sensitivity: Test different α weight scaling strategies to find optimal progressive guidance configuration

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out

### Open Question 2
- Question: How does 3DPX's performance generalize to 3D-to-2D reconstruction (generating PX from CBCT) and cross-modal image translation tasks?

- Why unresolved: The paper only demonstrates unidirectional 2D-to-3D reconstruction, leaving open whether the progressive hybrid MLP-CNN architecture can handle inverse tasks or translate between different imaging modalities.
- What evidence would resolve it: Experiments evaluating 3DPX's ability to generate PX images from CBCT data and comparing the quality to ground truth PX images using the same metrics (PSNR, SSIM, DSC) would demonstrate bidirectional capability.

## Limitations

- The exact architectural details of the Hybrid MLP-CNN Block remain underspecified, particularly the MAXIM implementation
- The method's performance on different anatomical structures beyond dental applications is untested
- Computational efficiency and inference time requirements are not characterized, limiting clinical deployment assessment

- **Low confidence**: Generalization to other 2D-to-3D reconstruction domains beyond dental imaging (no cross-domain validation performed)
