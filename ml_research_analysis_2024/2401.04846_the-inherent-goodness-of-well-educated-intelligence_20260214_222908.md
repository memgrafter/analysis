# The inherent goodness of well educated intelligence

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2401.04846
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.04846
- Authors: Michael E. Glinsky
- Reference count: 0
- One-line primary result: Proposes intelligence as understanding and controlling collective systems through knowledge of "the global consequences of local actions"

## Executive Summary
This paper presents a new theoretical framework for intelligence that emphasizes understanding and controlling collective systems through the lens of "twinkling textures" and their governing symmetries. The authors argue that true intelligence involves recognizing the global consequences of local actions and using this understanding to stabilize collective systems at optimal meta-stable equilibria. The framework introduces mathematical tools including the Heisenberg Scattering Transformation and Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman equation to characterize and control these systems through ponderomotive rather than viscous methods.

## Method Summary
The paper develops a mathematical framework for characterizing collective dynamics as "twinkling textures" governed by symmetries, then controlling these systems via ponderomotive methods. It introduces the Heisenberg Scattering Transformation to deconvolve collective patterns and the Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman equation to model optimal control strategies. The approach involves identifying meta-stable equilibrium points in collective systems and maintaining them through rapid vibrations rather than traditional viscous control methods, which the authors claim degrade performance by up to 90%.

## Key Results
- Collective systems can be characterized and controlled through identification of "twinkling textures" governed by symmetries
- Ponderomotive control can stabilize meta-stable equilibria without degrading system performance
- AI systems incorporating Heisenberg Scattering Transformation and Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman equation can effectively model and control collective behavior
