# Fashion Image-to-Image Translation for Complementary Item Retrieval
## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2408.09847
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.09847
- Authors: Matteo Attimonelli; Claudio Pomo; Dietmar Jannach; Tommaso Di Noia
- Reference count: 40
- Key outcome: GeCo achieves up to 0.85 AUC and 0.0307 MRR on FashionTaobaoTB, outperforming state-of-the-art baselines in top-bottom complementary item retrieval

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the challenge of top-bottom complementary item retrieval in fashion recommendation systems. The authors propose GeCo, a two-stage approach that leverages paired image-to-image translation within the Composed Image Retrieval framework. By first generating realistic bottom templates from input tops using a Conditional Generative Adversarial Network, and then integrating these templates for compatibility modeling, GeCo demonstrates significant improvements over existing methods. The approach is validated across three datasets, with particular success on the newly released FashionTaobaoTB dataset.

## Method Summary
GeCo employs a two-stage approach for fashion item compatibility modeling. In the first stage, a Conditional Generative Adversarial Network (CIGM) generates realistic bottom templates from input tops. The second stage integrates these generated templates with the original tops to form composite queries for compatibility modeling and retrieval. This method leverages the Composed Image Retrieval framework, where the generated bottom templates serve as a visual representation of the missing complementary item, enabling more effective retrieval of compatible fashion items.

## Key Results
- GeCo achieves up to 0.85 AUC and 0.0307 MRR on FashionTaobaoTB dataset
- Significant improvement over state-of-the-art baselines in top-bottom complementary item retrieval
- Generates higher-quality templates compared to existing methods, enhancing retrieval performance

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The approach works by addressing the fundamental challenge of visual compatibility modeling in fashion recommendation. By generating realistic bottom templates conditioned on input tops, GeCo creates a visual representation of what the complementary item should look like. This generative approach captures the complex visual relationships between fashion items that traditional feature-based methods might miss. The two-stage framework allows for explicit modeling of compatibility through the generated templates, which serve as queries that encode the visual characteristics needed in the complementary item.

## Architecture Onboarding
Component Map: Input Top -> CIGM Generator -> Generated Bottom Template -> Composite Query -> Retrieval Model -> Compatible Bottom Items
Critical Path: The most critical path is from input top through the CIGM generator to the retrieval model, as the quality of generated templates directly impacts retrieval performance.
Design Tradeoffs: The two-stage approach trades computational complexity for improved retrieval accuracy. The generative component adds overhead but captures complex visual relationships that simpler feature-based methods cannot.
Failure Signatures: Poor template generation quality (unrealistic clothing features, style mismatches) will lead to suboptimal retrieval performance. Dataset bias in training data may cause the model to generate templates that don't generalize to real-world fashion items.
First Experiments: 1) Generate templates from a diverse set of tops to assess visual quality and realism. 2) Compare retrieval performance using generated templates versus direct feature matching. 3) Test template generation and retrieval on held-out test sets to evaluate generalization.

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out
The paper does not explicitly call out open questions, but several areas remain for future investigation. The scalability of the approach to larger fashion item catalogs and real-time recommendation systems is not thoroughly addressed. Additionally, the paper relies on visual inspection rather than systematic human evaluation to validate template quality, suggesting a need for perceptual studies with fashion experts.

## Limitations
- Evaluation focuses primarily on retrieval metrics without extensive ablation studies to isolate the generative component's contribution
- Template quality claims are based on visual inspection rather than quantitative perceptual studies with human evaluators
- Scalability to larger fashion item catalogs and real-time recommendation systems is not thoroughly addressed
