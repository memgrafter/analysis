# DisCoRD: Discrete Tokens to Continuous Motion via Rectified Flow Decoding

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2411.19527
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.19527
- Authors: Jungbin Cho; Junwan Kim; Jisoo Kim; Minseo Kim; Mingu Kang; Sungeun Hong; Tae-Hyun Oh; Youngjae Yu
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: State-of-the-art FID scores of 0.032 on HumanML3D and 0.169 on KIT-ML

## Executive Summary
DisCoRD addresses the challenge of generating natural and faithful human motion by bridging the gap between discrete and continuous representations. The core method idea is to decode discrete motion tokens into continuous motion using a rectified flow decoder, which iteratively refines noisy motion with frame-wise conditioning features extracted from the tokens. This approach restores fine-grained dynamics and smoothness while preserving alignment with conditioning signals. The primary results show state-of-the-art performance with FID scores of 0.032 on HumanML3D and 0.169 on KIT-ML, along with a proposed symmetric Jerk Percentage Error (sJPE) metric that effectively captures sample-wise naturalness. DisCoRD achieves high naturalness without sacrificing faithfulness, outperforming both discrete and continuous baselines.

## Method Summary
DisCoRD introduces a rectified flow decoder to transform discrete motion tokens into continuous motion sequences. The method extracts frame-wise conditioning features from discrete tokens and uses these features to iteratively refine noisy motion through multiple steps of the rectified flow process. This decoding mechanism preserves fine-grained dynamics and smoothness while maintaining alignment with the conditioning signals. The approach effectively bridges the gap between discrete token representations and continuous motion generation, enabling high-quality motion synthesis that captures both naturalness and faithfulness to the conditioning input.

## Key Results
- Achieves state-of-the-art FID scores of 0.032 on HumanML3D and 0.169 on KIT-ML
- Introduces sJPE metric that effectively captures sample-wise naturalness
- Outperforms both discrete and continuous baselines in maintaining naturalness without sacrificing faithfulness

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The rectified flow decoder works by iteratively refining noisy motion using frame-wise conditioning features extracted from discrete tokens. This approach preserves fine-grained dynamics and smoothness while maintaining alignment with conditioning signals, effectively bridging the gap between discrete and continuous representations.

- **Motion representation conversion**: Understanding how discrete tokens map to continuous motion is crucial for the rectified flow decoding process
- **Iterative refinement**: The multi-step refinement process is essential for restoring fine-grained dynamics and smoothness
- **Frame-wise conditioning**: Extracting and utilizing frame-specific features ensures alignment between discrete tokens and continuous output
- **Flow-based generation**: The rectified flow mechanism provides a principled approach to motion generation from noisy initializations
- **Temporal consistency metrics**: Proper evaluation requires metrics that capture both distributional similarity and temporal smoothness

## Architecture Onboarding
**Component Map**: Discrete Tokens -> Feature Extractor -> Rectified Flow Decoder -> Continuous Motion

**Critical Path**: The feature extraction from discrete tokens and the iterative refinement process form the critical path for achieving high-quality motion generation.

**Design Tradeoffs**: The method balances between computational complexity (multiple refinement iterations) and output quality, while maintaining faithfulness to conditioning signals versus achieving naturalness.

**Failure Signatures**: Potential failures include loss of temporal consistency during refinement, misalignment between extracted features and motion dynamics, and sensitivity to hyperparameter choices in the rectified flow process.
