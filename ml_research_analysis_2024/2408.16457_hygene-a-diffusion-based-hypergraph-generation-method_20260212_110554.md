# HYGENE: A Diffusion-based Hypergraph Generation Method

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2408.16457
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.16457
- Authors: Dorian Gailhard; Enzo Tartaglione; Lirida Naviner; Jhony H. Giraldo
- Reference count: 40
- Primary result: Introduces HYGENE, the first diffusion-based method for generating hypergraphs that captures complex, high-order relationships

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces HYGENE, a novel diffusion-based method for generating hypergraphs that capture complex, high-order relationships. HYGENE generalizes iterative local expansion schemes and coarsening processes for hypergraphs using denoising diffusion models to progressively build hypergraph structure from minimal bipartite representations. The method achieves high validity rates (90% for ego hypergraphs) and demonstrates superior ability to capture underlying hypergraph structures compared to existing baselines across synthetic and real-world datasets.

## Method Summary
HYGENE generates hypergraphs by working on their bipartite representation, starting with a single connected node pair and iteratively expanding it using a denoising diffusion model. The method employs spectrum-preserving coarsening to maintain hypergraph spectral properties while reducing complexity. During expansion, it limits hyperedge clusters to at most three hyperedges to prevent excessive merging. The approach treats expansion numbers and edge existence as attributes of the bipartite graph, which are noised and recovered through the diffusion process. HYGENE is evaluated against multiple baselines including HyperPA, VAEs, GANs, and standard diffusion models.

## Key Results
- Achieves 90% success rate in generating valid ego hypergraphs
- Reaches 65% validity rate for SBM hypergraphs
- Demonstrates superior structural similarity metrics compared to baselines across NodeNumDiff, NodeDegreeDistrWasserstein, EdgeSizeDistrWasserstein, and Spectral measures

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: HYGENE achieves high validity rates by preserving hypergraph structure through spectrum-preserving coarsening and controlled hyperedge cluster sizes.
- Mechanism: The method builds a coarsening sequence that maintains spectral properties of the hypergraph via weighted clique expansion. During expansion, it limits hyperedge clusters to at most three hyperedges, preventing excessive merging that would lose structural detail.
- Core assumption: The spectral properties of the hypergraph are well-preserved when coarsening is done via weighted clique expansion and the preservation of k smallest eigenvalues.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "ablation studies confirm the importance of spectrum-preserving coarsening and limiting hyperedge cluster sizes during the generation process."
  - [section] "Controlling the merging of hyperedges (right-side nodes for the bipartite representation) is challenging... we select the contraction family to be the set of all pairs of adjacent nodes in the clique representation."
- Break condition: If the spectrum-preserving coarsening fails to maintain the hypergraph's spectral properties, the model may lose crucial structural information during the coarsening process, leading to poor generation quality.

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: HYGENE's iterative local expansion approach allows it to capture complex hypergraph structures better than one-shot or edge-by-edge generation methods.
- Mechanism: The method starts with a minimal bipartite representation and iteratively expands and refines it using a denoising diffusion model. This hierarchical approach first builds the global structure before refining local details.
- Core assumption: The iterative local expansion scheme is more effective at capturing complex hypergraph structures than one-shot or edge-by-edge generation methods.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "HYGENE works on the bipartite representation of hypergraphs, starting with a single pair of connected nodes and iteratively expanding it to form the target hypergraph."
  - [section] "Unlike regular graphs, where edges connect only two nodes, hyperedges can connect any number of nodes... the variable size of hyperedges and their higher-order relationships increase the difficulty of the task."
- Break condition: If the iterative process fails to correctly predict the expansion and refinement steps, the generated hypergraph may not accurately represent the target structure.

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: HYGENE's use of denoising diffusion models allows it to effectively learn the underlying distribution of hypergraphs.
- Mechanism: The method treats the expansion numbers for the left and right side nodes and the existence of edges as attributes of the bipartite graph. These attributes are noised, and a denoising model is trained to recover the original unnoised attributes.
- Core assumption: Denoising diffusion models are effective at learning the underlying distribution of hypergraph structures.
- Evidence anchors:
  - [abstract] "HYGENE works on the bipartite representation of hypergraphs... using a denoising diffusion process."
  - [section] "We employ EDM denoising diffusion framework [20] to model the probability p(vL, vR, e|B): vL, vR, and e are treated as node and edge features of the bipartite representation; these features are noised and a model is trained to recover the original features."
- Break condition: If the denoising diffusion model fails to learn the underlying distribution of hypergraph structures, the generated hypergraphs may not accurately represent the target distribution.

## Architecture Onboarding

- Component map: Input datasets -> Preprocessing (coarsening, bipartite conversion) -> Denoising diffusion model (PPGN) -> Generated hypergraphs
- Critical path:
  1. Compute coarsening sequences for input hypergraphs
  2. Train denoising diffusion model on coarsened bipartite representations
  3. Use trained model to iteratively expand and refine a minimal bipartite graph into a target hypergraph
- Design tradeoffs:
  - Tradeoff between preserving spectral properties and computational complexity during coarsening
  - Tradeoff between model complexity and generation quality
- Failure signatures:
  - Low validity rates (hypergraphs not satisfying structural requirements)
  - Poor performance on structural similarity metrics (NodeNumDiff, NodeDegreeDistrWasserstein, EdgeSizeDistrWasserstein, Spectral)
  - Inability to generate correct ego or SBM hypergraphs
- First 3 experiments:
  1. Verify that the coarsening process preserves spectral properties by comparing eigenvalues of original and coarsened hypergraphs.
  2. Test the model's ability to generate valid hypergraphs on the Ego dataset (high validity rate expected).
  3. Evaluate the model's performance on the SBM dataset (lower validity rate expected due to more complex structure).

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out
The paper does not explicitly call out open questions, but based on the limitations section, some implicit open questions include:

1. How does HYGENE's performance scale with hypergraph size, particularly for datasets with significantly larger numbers of nodes and hyperedges than those tested?
2. Can the spectrum-preserving coarsening algorithm be modified to handle cases where the desired reduction fraction cannot be achieved due to the constraint on hyperedge cluster sizes?
3. How would HYGENE perform if the denoising diffusion model architecture were replaced with other graph generation approaches like normalizing flows or autoregressive models?

## Limitations
- HYGENE's performance heavily depends on the effectiveness of spectrum-preserving coarsening, which is not extensively validated in the hypergraph domain
- The 65% validity rate on SBM hypergraphs indicates potential limitations in capturing complex stochastic block structures
- The method requires hypergraph coarsening sequences as input, which may not be readily available for all real-world hypergraphs

## Confidence
- High confidence in the denoising diffusion model implementation (based on established EDM framework)
- Medium confidence in spectrum-preserving coarsening effectiveness for hypergraphs (limited prior work in this specific application)
- Medium confidence in the iterative expansion approach (novelty makes empirical validation critical)
