# Classification of Geological Borehole Descriptions Using a Domain Adapted Large Language Model

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2407.10991
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.10991
- Authors: Hossein Ghorbanfekr; Pieter Jan Kerstens; Katrijn Dirix
- Reference count: 40
- One-line primary result: Domain-adapted BERT model (GEOBERTje) achieves 0.94 classification accuracy for main lithology in Dutch geological borehole descriptions

## Executive Summary
This study introduces GEOBERTje, a domain-adapted large language model specifically trained on Dutch geological borehole descriptions from Flanders, Belgium. The model addresses the challenge of extracting structured lithology information from unstructured textual descriptions by leveraging transfer learning and fine-tuning techniques. GEOBERTje successfully classifies borehole descriptions into main, secondary, and tertiary lithology classes, demonstrating superior performance compared to both traditional rule-based approaches and general-purpose models like GPT-4.

The research showcases how domain-specific adaptation of large language models can significantly enhance the accuracy and efficiency of geological data analysis. By transforming unstructured geological descriptions into structured, analyzable formats, GEOBERTje opens new avenues for geological analysis and modeling. The high classification accuracy (0.94 for main lithology) demonstrates the practical value of this approach for geological survey organizations and researchers working with textual borehole data.

## Method Summary
The researchers developed GEOBERTje by adapting a pre-trained BERT model to the domain of geological borehole descriptions. They fine-tuned the model using a dataset of Dutch geological borehole descriptions from Flanders, Belgium, focusing on lithology classification tasks. The model was trained to classify descriptions into main, secondary, and tertiary lithology classes, transforming unstructured textual data into structured numeric vectors. The domain adaptation process involved training on specialized geological terminology and contextual patterns specific to borehole descriptions, enabling the model to capture the nuances of geological language.

## Key Results
- GEOBERTje achieves 0.94 classification accuracy for main lithology, significantly outperforming traditional rule-based approaches and GPT-4
- The model successfully extracts lithology information from unstructured borehole descriptions and represents it as structured numeric vectors
- Domain adaptation proves essential, with GEOBERTje showing superior performance specifically on Dutch geological borehole descriptions from Flanders

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
Domain adaptation allows the model to learn specialized geological terminology and contextual patterns that general-purpose language models may not capture effectively. By fine-tuning on domain-specific data, GEOBERTje develops a deeper understanding of geological language nuances, enabling more accurate classification of lithology descriptions. The transfer learning approach leverages pre-existing language understanding capabilities while focusing on the specific challenges of geological text analysis.
