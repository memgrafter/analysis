# Retrieval-Augmented Diffusion Models for Time Series Forecasting

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2410.18712
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.18712
- Authors: Jingwei Liu; Ling Yang; Hongyan Li; Shenda Hong
- Reference count: 40
- Key outcome: Introduces RATD, a retrieval-augmented diffusion model for time series forecasting, achieving MSE of 0.013 on the Exchange dataset

## Executive Summary
This paper addresses the instability issues in existing time series diffusion models by introducing a Retrieval-Augmented Time series Diffusion model (RATD). RATD incorporates an embedding-based retrieval process and a reference-guided diffusion model that leverages relevant time series samples from a database to guide the denoising process. The approach aims to maximize the utilization of meaningful samples within the dataset while compensating for guidance deficiencies in traditional diffusion models.

## Method Summary
RATD consists of two main components: an embedding-based retrieval module and a reference-guided diffusion model. The retrieval module uses time series embeddings to identify relevant samples from a database, which serve as references during the denoising process. The diffusion model then incorporates these references to stabilize and improve the forecasting process. This approach leverages the meaningful patterns found within the dataset to enhance the guidance during diffusion, addressing the instability issues commonly found in existing time series diffusion models.

## Key Results
- Achieves MSE of 0.013 on the Exchange dataset
- Achieves MAE of 0.073 on the Exchange dataset
- Achieves CRPS of 0.339 on the Exchange dataset
- Demonstrates effectiveness particularly in complex prediction tasks
