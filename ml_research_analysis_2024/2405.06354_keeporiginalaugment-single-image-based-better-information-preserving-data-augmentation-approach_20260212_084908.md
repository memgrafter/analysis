# KeepOriginalAugment: Single Image-based Better Information-Preserving Data Augmentation Approach

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2405.06354
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.06354
- Reference count: 40
- Key result: Achieves 2% absolute error rate improvement on CIFAR-100 with PreActResNet-101

## Executive Summary
This paper introduces KeepOriginalAugment, a novel data augmentation approach designed to overcome the limitations of existing techniques like SalfMix and KeepAugment. The method intelligently incorporates the most salient region within non-salient areas while allowing augmentation to be applied to either region, striking a balance between data diversity and information preservation. Experimental evaluations on CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and TinyImageNet datasets demonstrate superior performance compared to state-of-the-art techniques, with an absolute 2% improvement in error rate for CIFAR-100 when using the PreActResNet-101 architecture.

## Method Summary
KeepOriginalAugment uses saliency detection to identify important image regions, then incorporates the salient region within non-salient areas before applying augmentation. The method explores three placement strategies (minimum, maximum, or random area) for the salient region and investigates which part (salient or non-salient) should undergo augmentation. During training, a 50% probability trade-off ensures both original and augmented data are used, preventing domain shift while maintaining data diversity. The approach applies RandAugment to selected regions and combines them with original image components.

## Key Results
- Achieves 2% absolute error rate improvement on CIFAR-100 with PreActResNet-101
- Outperforms existing state-of-the-art techniques like SalfMix and KeepAugment
- Effectively leverages diverse salient and non-salient regions without compromising information preservation
- Shows consistent improvements across CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and TinyImageNet datasets

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: Random placement of salient regions within non-salient areas introduces scaling data augmentation that enhances diversity
- Mechanism: Random selection of one of eight surrounding regions for salient region placement ensures different scaling effects across training epochs, preventing overfitting to fixed salient region size or position
- Core assumption: Scaling effect of resizing salient region to fit different area sizes provides meaningful variation
- Evidence anchors: [abstract] "We explore three strategies for determining the placement of the salient region—minimum, maximum, or random—and investigate swapping perspective strategies to decide which part (salient or non-salient) undergoes augmentation"

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: Augmenting both salient and non-salient regions while maintaining 50% probability of original data prevents domain shift
- Mechanism: Applies augmentation to both regions separately, then combines them with probability trade-off between original and augmented samples
- Core assumption: Balanced mix of original and augmented data maintains data distribution while providing sufficient diversity
- Evidence anchors: [abstract] "allowing augmentation to be applied to either region"

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: Incorporating salient region within non-salient area preserves contextual information
- Mechanism: Places salient region inside non-salient region before augmentation, maintaining spatial relationship and contextual information
- Core assumption: Spatial relationship between salient and non-salient regions contains important contextual information
- Evidence anchors: [abstract] "KeepAugment, which selectively preserves salient regions and augments non-salient ones, introduces a domain shift that hinders the exchange of crucial contextual information"

## Limitations

- Method specifically validated on small-scale datasets (CIFAR-10/100, TinyImageNet) with limited image sizes
- Fixed 50% probability trade-off not extensively explored for optimal settings across different scenarios
- Computational overhead from saliency detection and dual-region augmentation not thoroughly analyzed

## Confidence

- Mechanism 1 (Random Placement Diversity): Medium confidence - concept is sound but lacks direct empirical validation for scaling effects
- Mechanism 2 (50% Probability Balance): Low-Medium confidence - theoretically reasonable but no ablation studies on probability sensitivity
- Mechanism 3 (Contextual Information Preservation): Medium confidence - intuitive but lacks quantitative analysis of preserved contextual information
