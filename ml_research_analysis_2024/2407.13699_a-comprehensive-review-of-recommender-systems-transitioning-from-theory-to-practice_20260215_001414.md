# A Comprehensive Review of Recommender Systems: Transitioning from Theory to Practice

## Quick Facts
- arXiv ID: 2407.13699
- Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.13699

## Executive Summary
This survey comprehensively reviews recommender systems from 2017 to 2024, bridging the gap between theoretical research and practical applications. It systematically covers foundational techniques like collaborative filtering and content-based filtering, then progresses to advanced methods including deep learning, graph-based models, reinforcement learning, and large language models. The paper examines specialized systems addressing context-awareness, fairness, and domain-specific challenges across e-commerce, healthcare, and finance sectors.

## Method Summary
The authors conducted a systematic literature review using multiple databases (IEEE Xplore, ACM Digital Library, PubMed, ScienceDirect, JMLR, Wiley) to collect papers from 2017-2024. They applied specific search queries covering various aspects of recommender systems, screened papers using inclusion/exclusion criteria, and categorized them by domain and year. The review process included quality assessment questions and aimed to identify trends, challenges, and applications across different RS domains through detailed analysis of collected papers.

## Key Results
- Successfully categorized RS methods from foundational to advanced, including deep learning, graph-based models, and large language models
- Mapped theoretical advances to practical applications across e-commerce, healthcare, and finance sectors
- Provided detailed tables linking RS types to datasets, evaluation metrics, model accuracy, and practical applications
